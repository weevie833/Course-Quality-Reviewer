Overview: The (Il)Logic Behind Grant Writing

Grant writing straddles the line between science and art, as often times grant writers are tasked with teasing out the goals and objectives of a particular project from staff who do not necessarily think in those terms. In other words, program and project staff often approach grant writers or the executive director and express a need for something. A grant is seen as a solution to this need.

But is it? This is an important question that serves as the beginning of what could be characterized as an investigation into the nature of the request to write a grant. While grant writers may never be asked to necessarily write an organization’s strategic plan, their success hinges on the ability to understand how a grant fits into an organization’s overall strategy.

The logic of any grant request can be distilled into a project or program’s inputs, actions, outputs, and short and long-term outcomes. In theory, grant narratives break down the cause-and-effect relationship between a project or program’s activities and the change(s) expected as their result. This logic, however, is not the same for all kinds of organizations, projects, or programs, which is a distinction that is also critical to understand before beginning to write any grant.
