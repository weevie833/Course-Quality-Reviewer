Overview: Grant Deconstructed

Grants are carefully constructed arguments that lay out the reasons why a potential funder should consider making a financial investment in a project or program. Aside from the reasons for why a project or program represents a good investment, well-written grants also communicate organizational capacity. This capacity can be demonstrated in a grant through multiple levels of an organization, including governance, projects/programs, and staffing.

Grants are not documents written for the sole purpose of demonstrating a need and requesting support. Rather, grants generally communicate specific strategies, objectives, goals, and outcomes as they relate to a particular project/program and the organization itself. In effect, grants serve as granular blueprints for the who, what, where, how, and why behind critical aspects of an organization’s mission—all of which are captured within the structural elements of the grant narrative itself.
