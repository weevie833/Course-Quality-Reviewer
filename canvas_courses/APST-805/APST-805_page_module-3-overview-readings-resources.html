Overview: Prospecting Possible Funders

Successful grant writing rests on constructing written arguments that answer the capabilities and wants of potential public and private sector funding sources. These arguments hinge on the development of an understanding of the linkage between an organization’s needs and mission and a funding source’s capacity and philanthropic strategies.

To develop such an understanding, it is necessary to collect and analyze data on potential funding sources. Some attributes of potential funders into which research should be conducted include the nature of the funding source, past giving behaviors, existent relationships, and philanthropic interests. 

While online funding databases are an absolute necessity for a grant writer, there are other methods that may be employed to ‘tease out’ prospects. The balance that must be achieved in this process is between what is known and unknown. Effective searches into possible funding sources must therefore be carefully constructed to yield useful results, yet not based on logic that limits the number of results due to unfounded assumptions about the nature of prospective funders.

In this module, you will use a "Grant Research Prospect Tracking Sheet." This sheet enables a grant writer to track prospects, their respective funding priorities, and grant deadlines. A document or sheet like this allows you to "track" your prospects. As you conduct your research, you should be guided by your understanding of the need your Grant Narrative is attempting to address.
