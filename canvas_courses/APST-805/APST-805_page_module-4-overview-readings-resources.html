Overview: Supporting the Grant Narrative

One of the biggest challenges for a grant writer is how to navigate the choppy waters of background documentation. From budgets and financial statements to statements of support and 501c3 letters, grant writers are administrators as much as they are writers. 

These waters are often made choppier for several reasons. First, the organization may not possess the documentation that is requested by the prospective funder. Two, the organization may not necessarily understand the nature of the documents that have been requested. For instance, many nonprofits may not necessarily have a full-time bookkeeper or accountant on staff, which can cause unexpected challenges when financial documents are requested. 

Lastly, these waters can be made particularly unsettled if the organization simply does not have an answer to a question. For example, not all nonprofits have a written strategic plan with specific goals and objectives. This does not mean that the nonprofit is not worthy of funding, but it does call into question the logistics surrounding how a grant narrative is to be written (or managed).

One idea to keep in mind is that your request for financial support from a prospective funder extends beyond a narrative. Your request for support, then, is itself supported by a host of background documents.
