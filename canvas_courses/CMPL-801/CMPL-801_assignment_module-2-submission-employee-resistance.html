Overcoming Resistance to a New Information System

Context:
Your current or future employer is preparing to implement a new information system that all employees will be expected to adopt quickly. Successful adoption will be essential for aligning IT capabilities with the organization's strategic goals.

Assignment:
Write a one-page outline of a strategic plan for overcoming employee resistance to this new system. Your outline should be clear, actionable, and aligned with principles of IT planning and organizational strategy.

Your plan should include the following elements:

  1. Connection to Vision and Objectives
    • Briefly describe how the new information system supports the company's vision, mission, or strategic objectives.
    • Identify one or two measurable goals (objectives and key results) for the system's implementation.
  2. Communication Strategy
    • Outline how you will communicate the purpose and benefits of the new system to employees.
    • Consider how the system creates value for both the organization and individual users.
  3. Adoption Drivers and Obstacles
    • List key drivers that will help encourage user adoption (e.g., training, incentives, leadership support).
    • Identify likely obstacles (e.g., lack of familiarity, fear of change, insufficient support) and how you would address them.
  4. Training and Support Plan
    • Describe what initial and ongoing training or resources will be provided.
    • Suggest how managers and IT staff can reinforce learning and ease the transition.

Length: Approximately one page (outline or bulleted format is acceptable)

Goal: Apply your understanding of strategic planning and IT alignment to address the real-world challenge of user adoption in an organizational context.
