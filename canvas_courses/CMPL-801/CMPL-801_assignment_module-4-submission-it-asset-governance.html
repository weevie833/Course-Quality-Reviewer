Assignment: Governance and Protection Policies for IT Assets

In this assignment, you will design a governance framework for your hypothetical IT department, focusing on policies that govern, protect, and ensure compliance for the company's IT assets. Each policy must be well-defined, comprehensive, and demonstrate alignment with relevant federal regulations. This assignment will form Section 3: Governance of your final business proposal.

Assignment Components:

1 - Introduction (Half a Page)

  • Begin by introducing the purpose of IT governance and protection policies in ensuring business continuity, compliance, and data integrity.
  • Briefly mention the importance of adhering to federal regulations (e.g., HIPAA, GDPR, SOX) in safeguarding IT assets.
  • Provide a high-level overview of the four policies (IT governance, risk management, security processes, and computer use) that will be included.

2 - IT Governance Policy (1.5–2 Pages)

  • Purpose: Explain the role of IT governance in aligning IT strategies with business goals and ensuring accountability.
  • Policy Description:
    • Define how IT projects will be prioritized, monitored, and evaluated.
    • Specify leadership roles (e.g., CIO, IT steering committee) and decision-making processes.
    • Outline processes for IT resource allocation, change management, and performance measurement.
  • Compliance Measures: Detail how adherence to governance standards will be monitored, such as regular audits, performance reviews, and alignment with frameworks like COBIT or ITIL.

3 - Risk Management Policy (1.5–2 Pages)

  • Purpose: Address the identification, assessment, and mitigation of IT-related risks.
  • Policy Description:
    • Outline the steps for risk assessment (e.g., identifying vulnerabilities, calculating risk impact).
    • Detail proactive measures, such as implementing disaster recovery plans and conducting regular system backups.
    • Include a strategy for responding to and recovering from IT incidents.
  • Compliance Measures: Explain how the policy will be reviewed and updated regularly using risk assessments, incident reports, and regulatory compliance checks (e.g., NIST Cybersecurity Framework).

4 - Security Processes Policy (1.5–2 Pages)

  • Purpose: Ensure IT systems and data are secure against internal and external threats.
  • Policy Description:
    • Define access control measures (e.g., multi-factor authentication, role-based access).
    • Describe how data encryption and intrusion detection systems will be implemented.
    • Include ongoing employee training and awareness programs about cybersecurity best practices.
  • Compliance Measures: Detail how security processes will be audited and tested, such as conducting penetration testing, reviewing logs, and aligning with regulations like GDPR, PCI DSS, or CCPA.

5 - Computer Use Policy (1.5–2 Pages)

  • Purpose: Set clear expectations for employee behavior regarding the use of company IT assets.
  • Policy Description:
    • Define acceptable and unacceptable uses of company devices, networks, and software.
    • Include guidelines on email use, internet access, and the handling of sensitive information.
    • Address consequences for policy violations (e.g., disciplinary actions, revocation of access).
  • Compliance Measures: Specify how compliance will be ensured through regular training, monitoring of user activities, and periodic evaluations of usage logs.

Formatting Guidelines:

  • Length: 6–8 pages (each policy should be on its own page).
  • Structure: Use headings and subheadings for clarity. Include an introduction, a detailed description for each policy, and a section on how compliance will be measured.
  • Tone: Maintain a professional and formal tone throughout.
  • References: Cite any frameworks, regulations, or external sources used in APA 7th edition format.
  • Submission: Insert this document into your business proposal under Section 3: Governance.
