Overview

Today’s IT environments are becoming increasingly complex. The globalization of business, the continued rapid technology advances, and remote work locations have all contributed to the possibility of misuse of corporate IT assets and data, be it accidental due to a lack of knowledge, or a deliberate act of harm.  Should these acts result in data breaches, the business and its employees face certain legal and ethical obligations to inform not only the affected consumers but also certain regulatory bodies as well.

Last week you learned about the services a typical IT department provides a business. This week you will read about the IT department’s role in providing secure information systems and the individual responsibilities required to manage them.  You will explore the concepts differentiating ethical codes from legal codes, and how a violation of either affects a business’s public goodwill.

Module Objectives:

  • Identify the factors contributing to IT complexity
  • Understand the risks of IT asset misuse
  • Explore legal and ethical responsibilities
  • Discuss the role of regulatory compliance
