Overview

Overview Chapter 4: Hardware and Software

This chapter explores the roles of fundamental computer hardware components and primary computer classes within organizations. It introduces green computing and its environmental benefits and covers the two main types of software—system and application software—and their subclasses. The chapter also highlights the advantages of commercial, off-the-shelf, proprietary, and open-source software for various business needs.

Overview Chapter 5: Database Systems and Database Management

This chapter highlights the importance of quality data and information in driving effective decision-making and organizational success. It distinguishes between data management and data governance, emphasizing their unique roles in ensuring data reliability and compliance. The chapter also introduces the fundamentals of databases, with a focus on relational databases and their defining characteristics, providing a foundation for understanding modern data storage and organization.

Overview Chapter 13: Secure Information Systems

This chapter focuses on safeguarding organizational assets by exploring the CIA security triad (confidentiality, integrity, availability) and its application in mitigating cyber and non-cyber threats. It examines the process of creating a business impact assessment to identify critical vulnerabilities and outlines common cybersecurity threats. The chapter also explains the importance of information security policy audits, how organizations respond to security incidents, and the roles of symmetrical and asymmetrical encryption in protecting sensitive data.

Module Objectives:

  • Analyze the core components of computer systems
  • Evaluate software options and green computing
  • Assess the importance of data quality and governance
  • Explore database fundamentals and relational structures
  • Apply security principles to safeguard information
  • Analyze threats and incident response strategies
