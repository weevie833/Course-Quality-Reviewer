Overview Chapter 14: Corporate and Individual Accountability: Ethical, Legal, and Social Issues

This chapter explores the distinctions between morals, ethics, and laws, providing a foundation for understanding how they guide behavior in personal and professional contexts. It outlines steps organizations can take to safeguard customer data and examines the similarities and differences between government and corporate surveillance practices. The chapter also discusses measures to protect freedom of expression in the digital age and highlights the characteristics of high-quality information systems, including the importance of safety-critical systems in ensuring reliability and security.

Module Objectives:

  • Differentiate between morals, ethics, and laws
  • Evaluate data protection and surveillance practices
  • Assess freedom of expression and system reliability
