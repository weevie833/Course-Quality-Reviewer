Scenario:
You are the IT Operations Manager at a regional healthcare insurance provider. The executive team has approved the implementation of Robotic Process Automation (RPA) to improve operational efficiency, reduce human error, and free up staff for higher-value tasks.

The first phase of the rollout focuses on automating claims processing and data entry, two areas that are currently time-consuming and error-prone. Your team is responsible for managing the implementation—from identifying automation candidates and selecting an RPA tool, to training staff and maintaining the bots once they’re deployed.

Midway through the pilot program, you encounter several challenges:

  • Some employees are resistant to the changes, fearing job displacement

  • A few automated processes are producing unexpected errors due to poor data formatting

  • There’s confusion around who is responsible for monitoring bot performance and handling exceptions

Discussion Prompt:
Based on the scenario above, respond to the following:

  1. Implementation Strategy: What steps would you take to ensure a smooth and successful RPA rollout? How would you prioritize which processes to automate first?

  2. Change Management: How would you address staff resistance and promote a positive culture around automation?

  3. Governance and Oversight: What policies or practices would you put in place to ensure ongoing bot performance, accountability, and error management?
