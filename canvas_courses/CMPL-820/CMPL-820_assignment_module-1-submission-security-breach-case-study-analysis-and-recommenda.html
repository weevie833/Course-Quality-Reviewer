Case Study Analysis Instructions:

Step 1: Choose a Security Breach

  • Start by picking a security breach that really made an impact—something that caught the public's attention and had serious consequences for the organization or industry involved.

Step 2: Break Down the Threats

  • Take a close look at what went wrong. What specific threats or vulnerabilities did the attackers exploit? Think about the bigger picture, like the state of cybersecurity at the time and any new tactics that were used.
  • Also, consider if there were internal issues, such as outdated policies or poor governance, that may have made the breach more likely.

Step 3: Assess the Organization’s Response

  • Analyze how the organization reacted to the breach. Did they have a solid incident response plan? How quickly did they detect, contain, and recover from the attack?

Step 4: Evaluate Their Security Measures

  • Look at the security measures that were in place before the breach. Were these measures effective in preventing or minimizing the damage?

Step 5: Offer Recommendations

  • Think about what the organization could have done differently to prevent the breach. Provide practical, evidence-based suggestions for improving their security. Consider the long-term strategy. How can they strengthen their overall cybersecurity? 
  • Don’t forget about compliance. Could following specific cybersecurity standards or regulations have helped them avoid the breach?

General requirements and instructions to complete your assignment

  • 4-6 pages in length.
  • Use APA v7 format for in-text citations and your reference page.
  • Type your responses in a Microsoft Word document.
