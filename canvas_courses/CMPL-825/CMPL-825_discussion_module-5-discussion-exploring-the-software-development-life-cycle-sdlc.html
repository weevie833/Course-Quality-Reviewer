Objective: This discussion assignment aims to help you engage in a collaborative exploration of the Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) by researching its key components and sharing insights with your peers.

Instructions: You will participate in an online discussion where you will research the SDLC, share your findings, and engage in thoughtful conversations with your classmates.

Step 1: Initial Post

Research the SDLC and compose a post that addresses the following points:

  1. Overview of SDLC:
    1. Provide a brief introduction to the SDLC and explain its purpose in software development projects.
  1. Phases of the SDLC:
    1. Describe the main phases of the SDLC (e.g., planning, analysis, design, implementation, testing, deployment, and maintenance).
    2. Highlight the key activities and deliverables of each phase.
  1. Methodologies:
    1. Choose one SDLC methodology (e.g., Waterfall, Agile, Spiral, DevOps, etc.) and briefly explain how it works.
    2. Discuss the advantages and disadvantages of this methodology.
  1. Real-World Application:
    1. Share an example of a real-world project or organization that uses the SDLC. Explain how the SDLC contributed to the success or challenges of the project.

Your initial post should be 300-500 words and supported by at least one academic or industry reference. Be sure to cite your source in APA style.

Step 2: Responses to Peers

After you have submitted your initial post, review the posts of at least two classmates and respond to their ideas. In your responses:

  • Compare and contrast the SDLC methodologies they discussed with the one you researched. How are they similar or different?
  • Offer additional insights on the phases or challenges of the SDLC.
  • Ask questions or provide feedback on their real-world example.

Each response should be at least 150 words.

Note: This assignment is designated Level 1 - No AI. The assignment must be completed entirely without AI assistance. The assignment must be produced relying solely on conventional research and study methods. AI must not be used at any point during the production of the assignment.
