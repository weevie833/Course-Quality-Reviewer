In this final module, you will submit your completed Capstone project and deliver a professional presentation that showcases the full scope of your work. Your presentation should reflect the standards of a graduate-level capstone experience, demonstrating clear articulation of your project goals, execution, challenges, outcomes, and alignment with program learning outcomes.

Presentation Requirements

Your final capstone presentation must meet the following requirements:

  • Length: 5–10 minutes
  • Format: Recorded video presentation using Kaltura. You may use other software applications to record your narrated slideshow presentation, but you must upload your video file using the Kaltura feature in Canvas.
  • Delivery: Professional, clear, and well-paced narration; use of visuals is strongly encouraged
  • Audience: Presentation should be appropriate for academic and professional stakeholders (e.g., faculty, peers, industry reviewers)

What to Include in Your Presentation

  1. Project Title and Overview – Introduce your project’s name, purpose, and background.
  2. Problem Statement and Objectives – What organizational or industry problem did you address?
  3. Development Lifecycle and Methodology – Describe your approach to execution, including planning, tools, and technologies used.
  4. Key Deliverables – Highlight major components of your project (e.g., system design, implementation features, documentation).
  5. Challenges and Adaptability – Describe any obstacles encountered and how you adapted to overcome them.
  6. Lessons Learned – Reflect on your personal and professional growth during the project.
  7. Program Learning Outcomes – Briefly explain how your project demonstrates mastery of selected program learning outcomes.
  8. Final Results and Impact – Share the final outcome of your project and its potential impact or next steps.
