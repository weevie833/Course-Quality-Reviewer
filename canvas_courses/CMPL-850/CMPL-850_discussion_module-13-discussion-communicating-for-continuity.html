As your Capstone project nears completion, your ability to communicate clearly through documentation becomes essential. In this discussion, you’ll reflect on how you’re translating complex ideas into usable guides and instructions for future users or implementers. Whether you’re supporting technical deployment or user adoption, effective documentation helps ensure the continuity and impact of your project. Choose one of the prompts below to reflect on your approach and gather insights from your peers.

Discussion Prompt

Choose one of the following to respond to in your initial post. Copy/Paste your selected option into your post so that your classmates know which option you selected.

Option 1 – Your Documentation Mindset: How are you approaching the documentation process for your project? Are you focusing more on technical clarity, user-friendliness, visual design, or something else? What’s been your biggest challenge so far?

Option 2 – Supporting Non-Technical Users: How are you ensuring that your end-user documentation is accessible to those without a technical background? Share any formatting, tone, or content strategies you’re using to make your materials approachable.

Option 3 – Documentation as a Leadership Tool: Documentation isn't just about instructions; it's also a form of leadership. How does your documentation reflect your ability to support, guide, or empower others through your work?

Peer Response Requirement

Respond to at least one classmate’s post. Consider offering feedback on their documentation approach, a suggestion for clarity or usability, or a connection to your own strategy. The goal is to share real techniques that support successful deployment and user experience.
