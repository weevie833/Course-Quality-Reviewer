Welcome to CMPL 850: Managing Information Technology Capstone!

In this course, you will complete an encapsulated IT Management project, meaning a structured end-to-end initiative that represents the type of strategic technology challenge an IT leader might face in a professional setting—but executed within a defined academic framework.

Think of this course as a real-world simulation, a project in which you're expected to plan, analyze, manage, and reflect on a technology initiative from start to finish. This might involve improving an IT process, managing a digital transformation effort, implementing a security policy, or solving a cross-functional IT-business challenge. What makes this “encapsulated” is that it's self-contained within the 16-week course structure: you’ll define the scope, develop the approach, track progress, and present outcomes within a single academic term.

Unlike a traditional research paper or exam-based course, your Capstone will draw upon everything you’ve learned across the program—leadership, risk management, project planning, security, and data strategy—but it will also require you to meet academic standards for analysis, research, and documentation. This means you'll apply graduate-level research methods, cite scholarly and industry sources, and submit reflective writing that demonstrates critical thinking and professional growth.

In short, this Capstone is both a leadership-driven technology project and an academic demonstration of your learning and readiness to lead in the IT field. You’ll finish the course not only with a grade but with a portfolio-worthy artifact that reflects your readiness to manage real-world IT challenges.

Program Learning Outcomes

  1. Implement organizational IT infrastructures, including integrated virtualization, cloud systems, and artificial intelligence, to enhance operational efficiency and drive innovation.
  2. Employ project management and leadership principles and ethical practices to effectively plan, execute, and oversee IT projects that align with strategic organizational goals and objectives.
  3. Apply business intelligence principles, big data analytics, and data privacy/security to inform strategic decision-making processes and mitigate information security risks.
  4. Employ professional leadership and teamwork skills that foster collaboration and cohesion within diverse IT teams to achieve project objectives, promote knowledge sharing, and achieve organizational goals.
  5. Evaluate organizational relationships to identify areas in need of improved performance and effectiveness.
  6. Develop theoretically grounded change management strategies that actualize an organization’s goals and mission.
  7. Utilize standard IT governance, strategic planning, and financial management principles to develop IT projects that enhance organizational growth, optimize investments, and ensure sustainability and regulatory compliance.
  8. Evaluate emerging technologies and innovative IT solutions for their potential to drive business growth and efficiency through adoption and integration into IT operations.
