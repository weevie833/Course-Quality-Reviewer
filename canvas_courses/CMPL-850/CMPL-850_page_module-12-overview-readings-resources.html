Overview

This module focuses on peer engagement, collaboration, and gathering feedback to strengthen your capstone project before final submission. As your work approaches its final stages, receiving input from fellow professionals can offer valuable insight into how your project is perceived in terms of clarity, innovation, and practical application.

You will complete two key assignments this week. First, you will present a component of your project in a short recorded demo or presentation, allowing your peers to provide constructive feedback based on what they see and hear. Second, you’ll design a structured peer evaluation survey to assess the perceived effectiveness and innovation of your project, helping you gather more targeted data for final refinement.

Through this collaborative sprint, you’ll not only sharpen your communication and presentation skills but also gain a better understanding of how your work is received by others in your field. This input will help inform your final revisions and give you greater confidence as you prepare for closure and portfolio completion.

Readings

This week's recommended reading should focus on the following online research.

  1. How to Design Effective Peer Feedback Surveys
  2. Giving and Receiving Constructive Feedback
  3. Presentation Techniques for Technical and Non-Technical Audiences
  4. Collaborative Leadership in Project-Based Teams
  5. Measuring Innovation and User Value in Capstone Projects
