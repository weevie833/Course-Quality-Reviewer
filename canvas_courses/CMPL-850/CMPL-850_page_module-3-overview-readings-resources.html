Overview

In Module 3, you will develop and submit the first draft of your Capstone Project Charter, a foundational document that formally initiates your project. The charter outlines the core rationale, structure, and boundaries of your work and serves as a critical checkpoint for clarity and alignment before full-scale execution begins.

This draft will include four essential components: a Needs Assessment that defines the problem or opportunity your project addresses, a Business Case that justifies its value, an identification of Key Stakeholders, and a statement of Scope, Goals, Constraints, and Risks. You’ll draw upon prior coursework, research, and organizational insight to create a professional, purpose-driven charter that aligns with graduate-level project planning standards. Feedback on this draft will guide revisions and set the stage for structured progress throughout the remainder of the course.

Readings

This week, review 2–3 professional or academic sources on project charters to guide the development of your own. Focus your research on the structure, purpose, and essential components of effective project charters. Be prepared to reference what you've learned when drafting your charter.
