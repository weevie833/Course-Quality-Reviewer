Module Overview

In Module 1, students are introduced to key organizational communication, branding, and crisis communication concepts. This module sets the foundation for understanding how leaders use communication strategies to shape organizational identity and manage public perception. The module also introduces students to their primary research project, which will be due at the end of the term. Students will begin to identify their research topic and engage with two pre-submission assignments that help scaffold the final paper.

The module includes three key activities: an infographic assignment for self-introduction, a discussion forum using a real-life crisis communication case study, and the selection of a research topic. These assignments encourage students to think critically about communication in leadership and begin laying the groundwork for their major research paper.

Module Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, students will:

  1. Understand the foundational concepts of organizational communication and how leaders use communication to shape branding and public perception.
  2. Analyze real-life crisis communication case studies to identify effective and ineffective communication strategies.
  3. Introduce themselves and their professional backgrounds through a visually engaging infographic, establishing connections between personal branding and communication.
  4. Identify a research topic for their final paper, focusing on a key issue or challenge related to leadership communication, branding, or organizational strategy.

Instructor Role:

  • Guide how to design a compelling infographic, offering examples or templates if needed.
  • Please encourage students to reflect on their leadership identity and values, linking this reflection to the broader personal and organizational branding theme.
  • Offer feedback on the infographic's content and design, focusing on how well students convey their personal leadership brand.
  • Guide students to select relevant and impactful crisis communication case studies, providing suggestions if necessary.
  • Facilitate discussion by encouraging students to think critically about the role of leadership communication in managing crises, drawing connections to organizational branding and reputation.
  • Monitor the discussion to ensure thoughtful engagement and challenge students to apply communication theories to their analysis.
  • Guide students in selecting a research topic that aligns with course themes and is manageable within the scope of the final paper.
  • Provide feedback on the relevance and scope of the selected topics, offering suggestions for refinement if necessary.
  • Please encourage students to choose a topic that will fulfill course requirements and contribute to their professional development and leadership growth. They may expand on this topic for their capstone research paper.

Conclusion

In this introductory module, students will engage with foundational concepts of organizational communication, branding, and crisis communication. The infographic assignment allows students to reflect on their branding, while the crisis communication case study discussion challenges them to analyze real-world communication strategies critically. Identifying a research topic helps students begin thinking about their final paper, laying the groundwork for future assignments that will develop their research and analytical skills. These activities will help students become more effective communicators and leaders in organizational contexts.
