Overview 

“The single biggest problem in communication is the illusion that it has taken place.” - George Bernard Shaw

Welcome to Module 1, an introduction to organizational communication.

Organizations are often complex, evolving, and dynamic landscapes. Leaders must be equipped with an understanding of organizational communication in order to bring out the best among staff, products and services, stakeholders, and a variety of other factors that influence how an organization operates.

All behavior is communication, and in this course we’ll explore fundamental principles, theories, and skills that will help equip leaders to facilitate effective organizational communication. I’m happy you’re here this term, and here’s to an exciting adventure together!

As we begin, here are a few online course management strategies that might be helpful:

  • Be sure to review the syllabus and orient yourself to the course and each week’s modules, assignments, and due dates
  • Set aside time at the beginning of each week to review required resources and take notes in whatever way works best for you
  • You may find it helpful to block time in advance each week for forum participation (both initial posts and responses to others) and completing assignments
  • Always ask! No question is too big or small, so please don’t hesitate to message me if you have course questions
