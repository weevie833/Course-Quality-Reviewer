Overview

“Very often, organizations are inflexible because there is too little communication between functions; they are too segregated.”

- Ken Robinson

Organizational communication isn’t a one-way channel. Even though organizations strive to maintain control via its communications efforts, teamwork, the roles emotions play in our interactions with one another, and conflict management are all critical components of organizational communication that leaders must understand.

Emotional awareness and mindfulness are essential skills for leaders to understand and practice in order to develop effective teams and manage conflict in ways that provide respectful solutions to common as well as organizational-specific issues.

The resources and assignments in this module are designed to help you identify ways to improve the quality of communication within an organization.

In addition, this week you’ll also be asked to identify and review two resources from the college’s Discovery Service, which will be a vital source of information for our final paper for this class and your capstone project. This activity also provides you with an opportunity to select resources that are of interest to you and describe them to your fellow learning colleagues – a critical leadership communication skill!
