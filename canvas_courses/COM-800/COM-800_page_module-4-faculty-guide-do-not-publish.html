In Module 4, students will explore the concept of organizations as communication systems and how communication shapes organizational culture, group membership, and workplace dynamics. The module includes three key components: a discussion post on how communication impacts organizational culture, a paper on marginal group membership, and a presentation on workplace bullying. These activities will deepen students' understanding of the complexities of organizational communication and its influence on group dynamics and workplace environments.

Module Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, students will:

  1. Understand the role of communication in shaping and maintaining organizational culture.
  2. Reflect on marginal memberships within groups and how communication affects inclusion or exclusion.
  3. Analyze the issue of workplace bullying and present strategies for preventing or addressing it through effective leadership communication.
  4. Apply communication theories to real-world organizational challenges, including group dynamics and workplace conflict.

Instructor Role:

  • Guide students to think critically about how communication influences organizational culture, emphasizing both top-down leadership and bottom-up informal communication.
  • Provide examples of organizations with strong or weak cultures and how communication contributed to these outcomes.
  • Monitor the discussion for thoughtful engagement and encourage students to connect their examples to communication theories and leadership strategies.
  • Guide students to critically reflect on their experiences or observations of marginal group membership, emphasizing communication's role in inclusion or exclusion.
  • Provide examples of marginal group dynamics in various organizational settings (e.g., workplaces, social groups, professional teams).
  • Please encourage students to connect their reflections to communication theories and leadership strategies that promote inclusivity.
  • Provide clear instructions on creating an engaging and professional presentation, including tips for effective slide design and presentation delivery.
  • Offer examples of workplace bullying cases and how communication strategies were used to address them, helping students understand how theory applies to real-world scenarios.
  • Provide feedback on both the content and delivery of the presentations, emphasizing the importance of clarity, professionalism, and actionable leadership strategies.

Conclusion

In this module, students will critically engage with organizations as communication systems, reflecting on how communication impacts culture, group dynamics, and workplace challenges such as bullying. Through the discussion on organizational culture, the paper on marginal group membership, and the presentation on workplace bullying, students will deepen their understanding of how communication shapes and reflects leadership practices within complex organizational systems. These assignments will challenge students to apply communication theories to real-world leadership challenges, preparing them to lead more effectively in diverse and dynamic environments.
