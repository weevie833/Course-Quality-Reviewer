In Module 5, students will delve into organizational culture and ethical communication within leadership contexts. They will engage in discussions centered around Edgar Schein’s article on organizational culture and the principles of ethical organizational communication. Additionally, students will complete a two-page assignment based on Gibb's work on communication and interpersonal climates, helping them explore the dynamics of communication in leadership settings.

Module Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, students will:

  1. Understand Schein’s model of organizational culture and its relevance to leadership communication.
  2. Analyze the importance of ethical communication within organizations, focusing on transparency, trust, and responsibility.
  3. Apply Gibb’s communication and interpersonal climates principles to organizational and leadership scenarios.
  4. Reflect on how organizational culture and ethical communication impact leadership effectiveness.

Instructor Role:

  • Guide the discussion by helping students understand Schein’s model and how it applies to real-world leadership scenarios.
  • Encourage students to think critically about how communication can shape or reflect an organization's deeper layers of culture.
  • Provide examples of leaders who have successfully shaped organizational culture through communication strategies, fostering a deeper understanding of how theory translates into practice.
  • Guide ethical communication, emphasizing principles such as transparency, accountability, and respecting diverse perspectives.
  • Encourage students to reflect on positive and negative examples of ethical communication and how leaders can implement communication practices that foster trust.
  • Offer feedback that challenges students to consider the long-term impact of ethical versus unethical communication in leadership contexts.
  • Ensure students understand the differences between defensive and supportive communication climates, providing examples or case studies if necessary.
  • Offer guidance on how to apply Gibb’s principles to real-world leadership scenarios, emphasizing the role of communication in shaping organizational climate.
  • Provide feedback on students’ examples, ensuring they critically reflect on the relationship between communication climates and leadership effectiveness.

Conclusion

In this module, students will engage with complex concepts related to organizational culture and ethical communication in leadership. By discussing Schein’s model of culture and principles of ethical communication, students will develop a deeper understanding of how leaders can shape organizational environments and maintain ethical standards in their communication practices. Additionally, through the assignment on Gibb’s work on communication climates, students will reflect on how communication styles impact interpersonal relationships and organizational success. This module provides a critical foundation for understanding the communication challenges leaders face and the strategies they can use to overcome them.
