Module Overview

In Module 6, students will explore critical topics related to power dynamics and intercultural communication. This module includes several key assignments: identifying and discussing articles relevant to the course content, writing a paper on French and Raven’s five forms of power, preparing an outline for their research paper due in Module 8, and completing an assignment on intercultural communication. These activities strengthen students’ understanding of leadership communication within diverse global contexts.

Module Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, students will:

  1. Identify and discuss academic and industry articles related to course themes, enhancing their ability to connect theory to practice.
  2. Analyze French and Raven’s five forms of power and their application to leadership and communication.
  3. Develop a comprehensive outline for their final research paper, synthesizing previous work and feedback from the course.
  4. Reflect on and analyze key concepts in intercultural communication, focusing on how to apply these concepts in leadership contexts.

Instructor Role:

    • Guide students to find relevant articles from credible academic or industry sources, emphasizing the importance of connecting theory to real-world applications.
    • Please provide examples of articles that align with course content and encourage critical thinking in their analysis.
    • Monitor the discussion for respectful and thoughtful engagement, ensuring students use course concepts to support their discussions.
    • Provide clear guidance on understanding and applying French and Raven’s power theory, offering examples that help clarify the concept for students.
    • Please encourage students to connect the theory to their leadership experiences or to case studies.
    • If necessary, offer feedback on drafts or outlines to ensure students engage with the concepts in depth.
    • Guide students to reflect on real-world leadership challenges they’ve encountered or read about that involve intercultural communication.
    • Encourage the use of communication theories covered in earlier modules to inform their analysis.
    • Offer feedback on how to connect intercultural communication strategies to leadership development.

Conclusion

This module provides students with a rich set of activities designed to deepen their understanding of leadership communication, power dynamics, and intercultural communication. By analyzing key articles, writing about French and Raven’s five forms of power, outlining their research paper, and reflecting on intercultural communication, students will strengthen their ability to navigate complex leadership challenges. These assignments lay the groundwork for their final research paper while equipping them with practical skills to lead effectively in diverse and dynamic environments.
