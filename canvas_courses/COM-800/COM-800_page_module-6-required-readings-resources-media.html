Overview

“No body of knowledge needs an organizational policy. Organizational policy can only impede the advancement of knowledge. There is a basic incompatibility between any organization and freedom of thought.” - William S. Burroughs

As we’ve already learned, organizational communication goes much deeper than official emails from the executive leadership team. It includes human beings at all levels, both within and outside of the organization.

In this module we’ll explore ways that resistance, empowerment, and politics are forms of organizational communication. We’ll also review the importance of diversity, inclusion, and equity in organizational communication and our roles as leaders.
