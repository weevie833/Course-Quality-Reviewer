This module focuses on Crisis Communication, an essential skill for leaders navigating complex situations in the workplace or public sphere. Students will engage in three key activities: a discussion on crisis communication strategies, attendance and reporting on a public meeting and developing a Crisis Communication Plan that includes an Executive Summary Presentation. These activities will enhance their ability to manage communication during crises, understand public communication dynamics, and formulate actionable communication strategies.

Module Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, students will:

  1. Understand key crisis communication strategies and their application in leadership.
  2. Analyze the dynamics of a public meeting and assess the effectiveness of communication during crises or high-stakes situations.
  3. Develop a comprehensive crisis communication plan, including an executive summary presentation highlighting the plan's critical elements for decision-makers.
  4. Apply communication theories and strategies to real-world crisis scenarios, focusing on leadership and stakeholder management.

Instructor Role:

  • Facilitate the discussion by encouraging students to draw on communication theories and crisis management principles covered in earlier modules.
  • Provide examples of effective and ineffective crisis communication to guide the discussion and ensure students understand the importance of leadership communication during crises.
  • Monitor the discussion for respectful and thoughtful engagement, especially when discussing high-stakes or emotionally charged real-world crises.
  • Guides identify appropriate public meetings to attend and offer suggestions for local or virtual events where relevant crisis communication is taking place.
  • Clarify expectations for the report, emphasizing the need to analyze the meeting’s communication dynamics rather than just summarizing the events.
  • Offer feedback on the reports, highlighting students' ability to apply communication theory to real-world situations.
  • Provide feedback on previous assignments to ensure students are well-prepared to develop a comprehensive crisis communication plan.
  • Offer a clear rubric for the communication plan and the presentation, outlining the expectations for content, organization, and clarity.
  • Provide examples of crisis communication plans or templates to help students structure their work.

Conclusion

This module challenges students to engage deeply with crisis communication strategies, understanding the theoretical underpinnings and real-world application of managing communication during high-stakes situations. Through discussions, attending public meetings, and developing a comprehensive crisis communication plan, students will refine their leadership communication skills, preparing them to navigate crises with clarity, transparency, and confidence.
