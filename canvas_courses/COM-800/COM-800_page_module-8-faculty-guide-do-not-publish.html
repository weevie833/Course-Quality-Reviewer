The final module in this communications course is designed to bring together the key learnings from the course and provide students with an opportunity to synthesize their knowledge through final research and reflection papers. The research paper builds on drafts and feedback provided in earlier weeks (Week 1, Week 2, and Week 6), while the reflection paper encourages students to evaluate their personal growth and insights from the course materials and how they apply them to leadership communication.

Module Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, students will:

  1. Submit a comprehensive, well-researched final paper integrating course concepts and communication theories.
  2. Reflect on their learning journey, identifying critical takeaways from the course and how these insights will shape their leadership communication practices.
  3. Synthesize feedback from previous drafts to improve and finalize their research paper.

Instructor Role:

    • Provide feedback on students’ draft submissions from Week 1 (topic proposal), Week 2 (literature review), and Week 6 (partial draft or findings).
    • Ensure students have incorporated feedback into their final submission, improving their analysis, organization, and use of communication theory.
    • Clarify any remaining questions on format or content leading up to the submission.
    • Encouragement for Future Growth: Encourage students to continue developing their communication skills as part of their leadership journey. Share additional resources (books, webinars, conferences) for continued learning in leadership communication.

Conclusion

This final module is a culmination of the course, requiring students to integrate the theories, strategies, and insights gained throughout the course into research and reflection papers. Students will demonstrate their analytical abilities and understanding of leadership communication by revising and finalizing their research papers based on earlier drafts and instructor feedback. The reflection paper provides a space for personal growth, allowing students to consider applying what they’ve learned in future leadership contexts. Through these assignments, students will solidify their knowledge and begin their next steps as leaders with improved communication skills.
