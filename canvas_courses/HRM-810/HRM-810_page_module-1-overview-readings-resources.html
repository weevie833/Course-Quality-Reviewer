Overview

This week introduces students to the foundational concepts of economic decisions and systems, economic activity, and the role of Human Resources (HR) within these frameworks. Students will examine how various economic systems—such as market, mixed, and planned economies, shape resource allocation and influence business operations. They will also explore the components of economic activity, including production, consumption, and key indicators like GDP and employment rates, to better understand the forces that drive business performance.

Through the respective learning activities, the intent is understanding the relationships between global economic environment to organizational practice, with a focus on how HR functions as a strategic partner. Through discussions, readings, and application exercises, students will analyze how HR adapts to economic shifts by managing workforce planning, compliance, and talent development. By the end of the week, students will be able to articulate the interplay between economic environments and HR strategy, laying the groundwork for more advanced study in human resource management.
