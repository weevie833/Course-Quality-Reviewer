Overview

This module focuses on how businesses operate within the global economy and their responsibilities to society and government. Students will explore the impact of globalization on trade, investment, and workforce dynamics, as well as how companies navigate diverse cultural, legal, and economic systems. The module also examines the social responsibility of businesses and governments, including ethical decision-making, environmental sustainability, and community engagement.

The role of Human Resources and Organization Development (HR & OD) is emphasized throughout, as HR professionals are instrumental in supporting global business strategies, managing culturally diverse teams, and ensuring ethical practices across international operations. HR also plays a critical part in aligning organizational policies with social and governmental expectations, particularly in areas such as labor standards, equity, and corporate citizenship.

Assignments this week include:

In this week’s assignments, you will explore the strategic role of HR in global business environments. Discussion #2 focuses on identifying three essential skills or traits such as cultural intelligence, adaptability, and effective communication for working successfully in multicultural or international settings. You will explain why each competency is important and suggest relevant training or development opportunities to build these skills. Then, engage with at least two peers by offering insights, examples, or experiences related to cross-cultural teamwork. Apply course concepts by analyzing the global market potential for a product or service in two or more countries. This includes discussing necessary adaptations, trade barriers, entry strategies (e.g., licensing or joint ventures), and the role of HR in supporting international expansion. Use textbook references, library resources, and other resources to enhance your analysis as appropriate.
