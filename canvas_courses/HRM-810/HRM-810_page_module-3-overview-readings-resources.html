Overview

This module introduces students to the foundational structures of business organization and the vital role of entrepreneurship and small business management in the economy. Students will explore the different types of business ownership—such as sole proprietorships, partnerships, corporations, and franchises and examine how organizational structure influences operations, decision-making, and growth potential. The module also focuses on the entrepreneurial process, from identifying opportunities to managing and sustaining small businesses in competitive markets.

The role of Human Resources & Organization Development (HR & OD) is integrated into these topics, highlighting how HR supports business formation and growth by recruiting talent, developing leadership, and building effective workplace cultures. HR is particularly important in small businesses and startups, where strategic hiring and organizational planning can make a critical difference in success and sustainability.

Assignments this week include:

  • Chapter 5 Assessments: Demonstrate the role of HR/OD in forming and sustaining operations in various business ownership types and organizational structures.
  • Discussion #3: Share insights and engage with peers on how entrepreneurship and small business development intersect with HR practices and strategic planning.

Through these activities, students will gain practical knowledge of how businesses are structured and launched, and how HR contributes to the success and stability of both new and established enterprises.
