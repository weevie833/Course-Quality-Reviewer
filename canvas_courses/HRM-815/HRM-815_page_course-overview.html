Welcome to HRM 815 - Employment Law and Ethics in Global Employment Practices

This course provides an in-depth examination of the legal, ethical, and cultural issues that arise in employment practices across the global business landscape. Through a comparative and applied perspective, we will explore topics such as U.S. and international employment laws, discrimination and harassment, employee privacy, dispute resolution, and corporate responses to global ethical challenges.

What You Can Expect Weekly:

Each week, you’ll engage with assigned readings, discussion forums, and application-based assignments. The course is designed to promote critical thinking and practical analysis through a combination of textbook cases, chapter questions, and collaborative discussions. Weekly expectations include:

  • Reading Assigned Chapters from the textbook and any supplemental resources
  • Initial Discussion Post due by Wednesday at 11:59 PM EST
  • Peer Response due by Sunday at 11:59 PM EST
  • Chapter Question Submissions or Case Analysis Exercises depending on the week

Major Course Assignments:

  1. Discussions (30%)– Weekly prompts require thoughtful engagement and application of course concepts with responses to peers.
  2. Chapter Questions (30%)– Weekly short-answer questions designed to reinforce understanding of key laws and their application.
  3. Case Analyses (3 total, 30%)– Short written analyses of textbook cases, requiring synthesis of legal knowledge and professional judgment.
  4. Final Presentation (10%)– A professional summary of major employment laws and their implications for your current or future organization.
