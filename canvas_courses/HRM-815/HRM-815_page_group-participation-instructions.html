Beginning this week, you will form groups for the final presentation in Module 8. Each group will consist of 3–4 students, functioning as a consulting team preparing an analysis of a major employment law issue.

Steps to participate in a Group:

  1. Microsoft Team channels will be created for each group.
  2. Introduce Yourselves: Once your group has been created, post a brief introduction in your group space, including your background, time zone, and preferred meeting times.
  3. Schedule a Preliminary Meeting: Each group should hold at least one introductory meeting this week (Module 4) to establish communication preferences (email, Canvas messages, Outlook, etc.) and brainstorm potential project directions. A how-to page is provided in Canvas with step-by-step instructions for setting up a group meeting using Outlook: Instructions for scheduling a group meeting using UNH's Outlook Calendar
  4. Confirm Roles: Decide who will serve as the initial group organizer (to coordinate meetings and ensure communication flows). Roles can rotate later.
  5. Preliminary Check-in: By the end of Module 5, each group should provide a short progress update (1–2 paragraphs in the group space) confirming that you have met and identified potential focus areas for the Module 8 project.

Why Form Groups Now?

The final project in Module 8 requires significant collaboration, analysis, and presentation development. Forming groups early ensures:

  • Adequate time for research and role assignment.
  • Stronger teamwork and cohesion.
  • Opportunities to seek faculty guidance on project direction before the final deadline.
