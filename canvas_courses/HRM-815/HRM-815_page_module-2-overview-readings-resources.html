Overview

In Module 2, we begin our exploration of employment discrimination and its implications for human resource practices, particularly during the hiring process. Understanding the legal foundations of equal employment opportunity (EEO) is crucial for ensuring fair and lawful recruitment practices. This week, we examine how employers must navigate federal anti-discrimination laws and regulations, including Title VII of the Civil Rights Act, when designing job postings, conducting interviews, and making hiring decisions.

We will explore the concepts of disparate treatment and disparate impact, as well as the burden of proof in employment discrimination cases. You will also be introduced to practical tools for avoiding discriminatory practices and ensuring your hiring procedures align with legal and ethical standards.

This module builds on the foundational legal knowledge introduced in Module 1 and prepares you to critically assess workplace decisions that may unintentionally create legal liability.
