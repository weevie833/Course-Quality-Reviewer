Overview

In Module 3, we explore the legal and ethical considerations involved in screening job applicants and employees. This includes the use of background checks, criminal history, employment eligibility verification, and various employment tests such as drug screening, cognitive assessments, and honesty tests.

HR professionals must balance the need to assess candidate suitability with the obligation to respect privacy rights and avoid discrimination. This module will help you identify the legal limits of employment screening and how to develop policies that are both compliant and fair.

Building on last week’s discussion about discrimination in hiring, this module highlights how screening tools—if misused—can lead to disparate impact claims and legal liability. You will apply legal reasoning to assess risk and propose ethical screening practices.
