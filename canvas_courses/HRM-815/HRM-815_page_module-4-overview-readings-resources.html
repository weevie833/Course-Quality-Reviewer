Overview

In Module 4, we examine some of the most sensitive and high-risk areas in employment law: harassment, affirmative action, appearance-based discrimination, and bona fide occupational qualifications (BFOQs). These topics are essential for ensuring inclusive, legally compliant, and ethically sound workplaces.

You'll explore how courts define harassment (including sexual and non-sexual forms), how liability is determined, and what steps employers must take to prevent and address inappropriate conduct. We'll also evaluate how personal appearance, gender expression, and weight can influence hiring and promotion decisions—often in ways that are legally questionable.

This module also introduces affirmative action and the legal use of BFOQs—two controversial topics where employers must balance legal requirements with fairness and business necessity. The knowledge gained this week will support deeper analysis of workplace rights, leave policies, and accommodations in Module 5.
