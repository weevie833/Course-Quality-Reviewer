Overview: Job analysis is a foundational element in building effective recruitment and selection systems. It informs the creation of job descriptions, competency models, and reward strategies that align with organizational needs.

Instructions: For this case study, choose either your current organization or another organization you are familiar with. You may also select a publicly known organization or use a hypothetical scenario inspired by real-world examples.

In a 2–3 page memo (double-spaced), address the following:

  1. Job Selection: Identify a specific job within the organization. Briefly describe the job and its context within the organization (e.g., department, reporting structure, importance to business goals).
  2. Job Requirements Analysis: Outline the tasks, duties, and responsibilities associated with the job. What knowledge, skills, abilities, and other characteristics (KSAOs) are needed to perform the job successfully?
  3. Competency Modeling: Identify at least three core competencies (e.g., teamwork, communication, adaptability) critical to success in the role. Explain how these competencies support organizational goals or values.
  4. Job Rewards Analysis: Describe the job’s intrinsic and extrinsic rewards. Consider pay, benefits, growth opportunities, and less tangible elements like work-life balance or organizational culture.
  5. Application to Staffing: Explain how the job analysis you’ve outlined could be used to improve recruitment, candidate evaluation, or retention efforts for this role.

Submission Guidelines:

Cite any sources used, including Staffing Organizations, in APA format.

Deliverable Format

  • Submit your work as a formal memo or short report addressed to a relevant stakeholder (e.g., Director of HR, Department Manager, or Executive Team) to address the respective questions.
  • Use clear, concise, and professional language, avoiding overly technical jargon.
  • Include headings and bullet points where appropriate to enhance readability.
