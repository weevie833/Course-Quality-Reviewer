Overview:
Your task in this case study is to evaluate how initial assessment methods are used to screen candidates and determine their potential for job performance. Using the concepts from Chapters 7 and 8 of Staffing Organizations, you will apply the principles of measurement quality (reliability, validity, and utility) and logic of prediction to a real-world or hypothetical hiring scenario.

Instructions:
Choose an organization you currently work for, have worked for in the past, or are otherwise familiar with. You may also select a public or hypothetical organization. Then, complete the following:

  1. Job and Hiring Context:
    • Briefly describe the organization, the job in question, and the context of the hiring process (e.g., number of applicants, urgency, internal/external factors).
  2. Initial Assessment Methods Used:
    • Identify the initial assessment methods used to screen applicants (e.g., resumes, application forms, reference checks, personality tests, structured interviews).
    • Discuss why these methods were selected and how they align with the job’s requirements.
  3. Evaluation of Measures:
    • Assess thereliability, validity, and utility of at least one assessment method used.
    • Were these methods legally sound and defensible? Why or why not?
  4. Logic of Prediction:
    • Explain how the organization attempted to predict applicant success based on initial assessments. Was the approach supported by evidence or intuition?
  5. Recommendations:
    • Based on what you've learned, recommend improvements or alternative assessment methods that could enhance the effectiveness and fairness of the selection process.

Length: 2–3 pages (double-spaced), APA format if citations are used. Cite any sources used, including Staffing Organizations, in APA format.
