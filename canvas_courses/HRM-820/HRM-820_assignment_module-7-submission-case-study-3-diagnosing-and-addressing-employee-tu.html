Overview: Employee Turnover can significantly impact an organization’s performance, morale, and cost structure. Effective retention strategies depend on understanding the root causes of turnover and implementing targeted solutions. This case study challenges you to analyze real or hypothetical turnover data and propose evidence-based retention initiatives.

Instructions: Choose one of the following options:

  • An organization you currently work for or have worked for in the past
  • A public organization with documented turnover issues
  • A hypothetical organization based on realistic industry data

In a 2–3 page analysis (double-spaced), address the following:

  1. Organizational Context
    • Briefly describe the organization and the role/department experiencing turnover.
    • Identify whether turnover is primarily voluntary or involuntary, functional or dysfunctional, avoidable or unavoidable.
  2. Turnover Analysis
    • What evidence (qualitative or quantitative) is available to explain the turnover problem? Consider exit interviews, retention rates, absenteeism, or performance data.
    • Are specific employee groups (e.g., tenure, job level, demographics) more affected?
  3. Cause Identification
    • Analyze potential causes of turnover. Consider job satisfaction, compensation, leadership, work-life balance, culture, or career development.
  4. Retention Recommendations
    • Propose at least two targeted retention initiatives. Explain how they address the identified causes and what outcomes you expect.
    • Include how success will be measured (e.g., retention metrics, engagement scores, cost savings).
  5. Connection to Course Concepts
    • Support your recommendations with concepts from Chapter 14 and other relevant modules.

Submission Guidelines:

  • Use APA formatting if citing external sources.
