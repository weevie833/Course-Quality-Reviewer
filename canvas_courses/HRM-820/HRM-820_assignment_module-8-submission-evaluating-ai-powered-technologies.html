Recruitment technologies such as HireVue, Pymetrics, Modern Hire, and Eightfold.ai are transforming how organizations attract and evaluate talent. These systems promise to streamline hiring by ranking resumes, analyzing video interviews, and even using AI to assess candidate behaviors and biometric signals. While they offer efficiency and data-driven insights, they also raise serious ethical and legal concerns about bias, transparency, and candidate experience.

As an HR leader, you may be tasked with evaluating these technologies and advising executives on whether, and under what conditions, they should be adopted.

Deliverable – Brief Report to the CEO

For this activity, prepare a 2–3 page professional report (double-spaced memo style or formatted brief) addressed to the CEO. Your report should:

  1. System Selection
    • Identify one recruitment technology system (e.g., HireVue or a comparable platform).
    • Briefly describe its target clients (industries, organization sizes, or use cases).
  2. Capabilities and Integration
    • Summarize the system’s main features (resume triage, video analysis, predictive analytics, etc.).
    • Explain how HR managers integrate it into the recruitment workflow.
  3. Value Proposition and Risks
    • Outline the benefits the vendor claims (efficiency, quality of hire, reduced costs).
    • Evaluate whether these claims align with principles of fairness, transparency, and ethical hiring.
    • Highlight any legal or reputational risks of adoption.
  4. Recommendation to CEO
    • State whether you recommend adopting the system, rejecting it, or adopting it with conditions.
    • If adoption is recommended, specify safeguards (e.g., bias audits, human oversight, candidate consent, manager training) that must be in place.
    • If declining adoption, explain under what future conditions (if any) the system might be reconsidered.
Note: This assignment is designated Level 1 - No AI. The assignment must be completed entirely without AI assistance. The assignment must be produced relying solely on conventional research and study methods. AI must not be used at any point during the production of the assignment.
