Scenario:

Imagine you are advising a mid-sized technology firm with 600 employees. The company is facing:

  • An aging workforce, with 30% of employees eligible to retire within the next five years.
  • A talent shortage in data analytics and cybersecurity roles.
  • Increased demand from employees for flexible/remote work options.
  • New state-level legislation requiring pay transparency in job postings and expanding protections against age discrimination.

In your initial post (300–400 words):

  1. Identify at least two major legal or compliance issues the company must consider in its workforce planning.
  2. Propose two workforce planning strategies that address both the compliance risks and the talent shortages/demographic shifts.
  3. Share a real-world example (from your own experience or research) of an organization that either succeeded or struggled because of how it approached compliance and workforce planning.
  4. Support your recommendations with course readings (Chapters 2 and 3) and at least one additional scholarly or professional source.

Peer Response:

Reply to at least one classmate by:

  • Comparing their recommended strategies to your own.
  • Identifying potential strengths or blind spots in their approach.
  • Suggesting an additional workforce planning tool or compliance consideration they could incorporate.

Why This Matters:

This activity will help you practice the real-world skill of integrating HR law and strategy. Employers must comply with regulations, but those who also anticipate workforce trends such as demographic shifts, skill shortages, and remote work demands are better positioned to remain competitive.
