The quality of staffing decisions depends heavily on the tools organizations use to measure and evaluate candidates. High-quality measures—those that are reliable, valid, and useful, help ensure that selection decisions are fair, legally defensible, and aligned with strategic goals. Initial assessment methods such as resumes, application blanks, and biographical data provide a first layer of screening, but their effectiveness depends on how well they balance efficiency, cost, and predictive accuracy.

Your Task (300–400 words):

  1. Select an organization you are familiar with (your current or past employer, a publicly known company, or one you research).
  2. Briefly describe the organization and a specific job role that is critical to its success.
  3. Explain why measurement is critical to the selection process for this role.
  4. Choose one initial assessment method (e.g., resumes, application forms, biographical data, or another tool) and evaluate its strengths and limitations in predicting job performance for this position.
  5. Recommend how the organization could design its selection process to balance cost efficiency, fairness, and predictive validity.
  6. Provide an example (from your chosen organization or research) where measurement influenced staffing outcomes either positively or negatively.

As you respond, consider:

  • Reference Chapters 7 and 8 and at least one scholarly or professional source.
  • How selection measures link to costs and implementation strategies.
  • How organizations can use industry-supported frameworks to guide workforce planning and decision-making in selection.
  • The risks of relying on low-quality or unvalidated assessment tools.
