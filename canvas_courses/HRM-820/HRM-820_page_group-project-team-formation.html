Final Group Project: Integrated Staffing Plan

Overview

The final group project serves as the capstone of this course, allowing you to synthesize and apply the recruitment and selection concepts covered throughout the term. Working in consulting teams of 3–4 members, you will act as staffing experts hired by an organization to design and present an integrated staffing plan.

Your project should demonstrate mastery of workforce planning, recruitment, and selection strategies that align with organizational outcomes and competitive goals. The project will also highlight your ability to evaluate staffing tools and practices, incorporate legal considerations, and present actionable recommendations for attracting and retaining top talent.

Project Components

Organization Selection and Proposal (Module 1)

  • Choose an organization (real or hypothetical) that will serve as the focus of your project.
  • Identify a staffing challenge or focus area that connects to organizational outcomes (e.g., workforce planning, candidate assessment, recruitment marketing, retention strategies).
  • Submit your topic proposal for instructor approval.

Written Report (Due: Module 8)

  • Executive Summary (1–2 pages): Provide a concise overview of your staffing plan, objectives, findings, and key recommendations.
  • Main Report (6-8 pages): Integrate course material, research, and organizational analysis to address:
    • Strategic alignment of staffing with organizational objectives.
    • Legal/regulatory considerations in recruitment and selection.
    • Job analysis and forecasting/planning.
    • Selection methods and evaluation metrics.
    • Retention and workforce planning strategies.
  • Formatting: Professional, well-organized, APA-style citations.
  • Presentation (Recorded, Posted in Module 8 Discussion)
    • Prepare a 10-12-minute recorded presentation summarizing your staffing plan.
    • Presentation should be clear, engaging, and professional, highlighting your most important findings and recommendations.

Expectations for Collaboration

  • Groups are expected to work collaboratively and divide responsibilities equitably.
  • Team-member evaluations will factor into individual grades.
  • Use project management tools (shared documents, meeting notes, timelines) to ensure accountability.
  • This project is designed to showcase your ability to apply theory to practice and to prepare you for real-world challenges in staffing and talent management.
