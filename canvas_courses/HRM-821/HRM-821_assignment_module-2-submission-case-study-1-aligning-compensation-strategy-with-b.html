In this case study, you will analyze a real-world scenario where an organization must make critical decisions about its compensation system to ensure alignment with business strategy and HR objectives. Building on concepts from Chapter 2 of Compensation (Gerhart, 14th ed., 2023), the case challenges you to evaluate how total compensation strategies can be designed to support long-term organizational success.

You will examine how strategic choices in compensation influence employee behavior, reinforce organizational culture, and create a sustainable source of competitive advantage. At the same time, you will consider the risks that arise when pay systems are misaligned with business priorities, such as decreased motivation, higher turnover, or reputational harm.

Your task is to apply theoretical frameworks and strategic models from the course to propose solutions that balance competitiveness, fairness, and cost-effectiveness. This case study is designed to help you connect theory to practice by assessing real strategic tradeoffs and developing actionable recommendations for HR leaders.

Deliverables should include:

  • Identification of the organization’s business and HR strategies
  • Analysis of the role of total compensation in supporting these strategies
  • Evaluation of the risks and opportunities created by current or proposed pay practices
  • Recommendations for aligning compensation with organizational goals to achieve long-term advantage

This activity encourages you to think critically about the relationship between strategy and pay, bridging classroom learning with applied decision-making in organizational contexts.
