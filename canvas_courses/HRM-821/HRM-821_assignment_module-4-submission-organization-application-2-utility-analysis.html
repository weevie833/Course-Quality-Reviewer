Utility analysis is a systematic method for evaluating the financial return of HR decisions, particularly those related to staffing, selection, and compensation strategy. Appendix 7-A of Compensation (Gerhart, 14th ed., 2023) provides a framework for estimating the costs and benefits of compensation changes by translating improvements in employee performance and reductions in turnover into monetary terms.

In this assignment, you will apply utility analysis to your own company or any organization you are familiar with. Your goal is to demonstrate how a compensation decision such as adjusting pay policy, implementing incentives, or modifying pay mix can be analyzed to determine whether the value created outweighs the costs.

Steps:

  1. Select a compensation-related decision for your organization (e.g., moving from lag to match market pay, raising starting wages, introducing performance-based pay).
  2. Using the format and structure provided in Appendix 7-A, estimate the potential impact of this decision in terms of:
    • Increases in productivity or performance
    • Reductions in turnover and associated hiring/training costs
    • Improvements in recruitment outcomes (quality and quantity of applicants)
    • Total financial return compared to the cost of implementation
  3. Complete your analysis by summarizing whether the decision results in positive, neutral, or negative utility for the organization.
  4. Conclude with recommendations for how managers and HR leaders could use this type of analysis to make evidence-based pay decisions.

Deliverable: A written response in a memo format to the CEO of the company (2–3 pages) that includes:

  • Utility analysis calculations and explanations following the format in Appendix 7-A
  • Discussion of the projected value and risks of the decision
  • Recommendations for organizational action based on your findings

This exercise will help you connect compensation strategy with measurable business outcomes and practice applying a quantitative tool that demonstrates HR’s impact on organizational success.
