Overview

In this module, we move deeper into the design of compensation systems by focusing on internal alignment and the role of job analysis in shaping compensation strategy. Drawing from Chapters 3 and 4 of Compensation (Gerhart, 14th ed., 2023), we will examine what shapes internal structures, the strategic choices involved in their design, and the distinctions between job-based and person-based approaches. Since the job-based approach is the most common, we will also explore job analysis procedures that help define roles, responsibilities, and pay structures.

This module connects the strategic foundation of compensation from Modules 1 and 2 to the practical design of internal structures. Through Discussion #3, you will evaluate how compensation strategy and internal alignment influence organizational effectiveness. In Organization Application #1, you will apply job analysis concepts to your own workplace or a familiar organization, reinforcing the link between theory and practice.

By the end of this module, you will be able to analyze how jobs and people are positioned within compensation systems and consider how alignment decisions shape motivation, fairness, and organizational outcomes.
