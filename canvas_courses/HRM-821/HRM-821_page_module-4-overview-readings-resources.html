Overview

In this module, we shift from internal alignment to external competitiveness, examining how organizations determine the pay levels and structures necessary to attract and retain talent in the labor market. Drawing from Chapters 7 and 8 of Compensation (Gerhart, 14th ed., 2023), we will explore the economic, organizational, and policy factors that shape external competitiveness.

Key topics include labor market factors, modifications to the demand side, and the strategic choices organizations make in specifying competitive pay policies. We will also examine how organizations move from policy to practice through the use of pay-policy lines, market pricing, and pay-mix decisions. Together, these approaches highlight how compensation strategies are shaped not only by internal goals but also by competitive pressures and external benchmarks.

In Discussion 4, you will evaluate how organizations balance internal alignment with external competitiveness and reflect on the implications of different pay policies. Organization Application #2 will give you the opportunity to analyze how labor market data and competitive positioning influence pay levels and mixes in practice.

This module will help you understand how external competitiveness impacts an organization’s ability to recruit, motivate, and retain employees while maintaining cost-effectiveness and strategic alignment.
