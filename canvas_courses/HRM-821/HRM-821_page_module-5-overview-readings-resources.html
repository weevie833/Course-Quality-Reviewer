Overview

In this module, we focus on the critical link between organizational strategy, compensation, and performance management. Drawing from Chapters 9 and 10 of Compensation (Gerhart, 14th ed., 2023), we will explore the theory and evidence behind pay-for-performance systems and examine the variety of plans organizations use to align employee behaviors with strategic objectives.

You will analyze how pay-for-performance can reinforce organizational goals, motivate employees, and enhance competitiveness, while also considering potential risks such as unintended behaviors, inequities, or administrative complexity. The module emphasizes designing performance-based pay systems that are strategically aligned, evidence-based, and fair.

Discussion 5 invites you to evaluate the effectiveness of pay-for-performance in different organizational contexts, while Case Study #2 challenges you to apply concepts by assessing a real-world scenario and recommending a performance-based pay plan that supports organizational success.
