Overview

In this module, we expand the discussion of compensation to focus on special groups—executives, unionized employees, and international workforces. Each of these contexts presents unique challenges that require balancing organizational strategy, external pressures, and fairness considerations.

Drawing from Chapters 14 and 16 of Compensation (Gerhart, 14th ed., 2023), we will examine the complexities of executive compensation, including incentive alignment, shareholder concerns, and ethical debates. We will also review the role of unions in shaping pay systems, collective bargaining agreements, and the balance of power between employees and management. Finally, we will analyze international pay practices, considering how cultural, legal, and economic factors affect compensation decisions in a global context.

By the end of this module, you should understand how compensation practices differ across these groups and contexts, and how HR leaders can design pay systems that are equitable, competitive, and strategically aligned.
