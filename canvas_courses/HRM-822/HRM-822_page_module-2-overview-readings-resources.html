Overview

Module 2 examines the foundational elements of talent management by exploring how employees acquire knowledge, develop skills, and demonstrate effective workplace behaviors. The module emphasizes the critical role of motivation in learning and performance, highlighting how individual and organizational factors influence employee engagement and development outcomes.

Students analyze key learning theories and their application within Human Resource Development (HRD), focusing on how learning and instruction can be designed to support meaningful skill development and behavior change. Attention is also given to learning styles and instructional strategies, enabling students to evaluate how different approaches to training can enhance learning effectiveness across diverse workforces.

Through textbook readings, discussion, and applied exercises, students connect theory to practice by examining how talent development initiatives can be aligned with organizational goals and employee motivation to improve performance.
