Assignment Overview

This course includes a team-based simulation - a hands-on opportunity to apply strategic human resource management concepts in a competitive business environment. Through this experience, students will move beyond theory and actively lead HR strategies, making data-driven decisions that influence organizational performance over time.

Working in teams, you will manage the full scope of HR responsibilities while competing against other organizations in the simulation. This exercise is designed to strengthen your ability to think strategically, analyze HR metrics, and align HR practices with business objectives, skills essential for senior-level HR leaders.

Preparation Requirements

Before the simulation officially begins, all students must complete the following:

  1. Register for the simulation and submit payment.
  2. Read all provided simulation materials and referenced help guides at the link above.
  3. Complete the required quiz within the platform.
  4. Run two (2) practice quarters to become familiar with the software and decision-making process.

Adequate preparation is critical. As in real-world HR leadership roles, poor preparation will negatively impact organizational outcomes.

Simulation Structure

  • You will manage an HR department for 12 quarters (3 simulated years).
  • The simulation will run on a scheduled basis throughout the term.
  • Each quarter requires strategic HR decisions related to workforce planning, compensation, training, performance management, and organizational effectiveness.
  • Your strategies and decisions directly determine your team’s success within a competitive market environment.

Decision Deadlines & Class Integration

  • Reviewing performance reports
  • Analyzing outcomes
  • Connecting simulation results to HRM theories and best practices
  • Discussing strategic adjustments for future quarters

Grading

This assignment is 30% of course grade and will be evaluated based on:

  • Quality and consistency of decision-making
  • Strategic alignment of HR practices
  • Use of HR data and reports
  • Team performance across all simulation quarters
  • Professional engagement and preparedness

Professional Expectations

This simulation mirrors the realities of managing HR in a competitive business environment. You are expected to:

  • Engage actively with your team
  • Meet all deadlines
  • Make informed, strategic decisions
  • Take ownership of your role within the HR department

Success in this simulation requires collaboration, analysis, and adaptability, all core competencies of effective HR leaders.

Assignment: Team Simulation Reflection (Managing the HR Function)

Purpose

This reflection evaluates your team’s HR decisions in the simulation, focusing on what strategies worked, where HR investments paid off, what could have been improved, and how outcomes connect to course readings. Your goal is to demonstrate strategic HR thinking using data from simulation reports.

Team Simulation Reflection Report (Written)

  • Length: 5–6 pages (APA 7)
  • Must use: simulation reports/metrics + course readings

Required Sections

  1. Executive Summary
    Overall results and key takeaways.
  2. HR Strategy Overview
    What approach your team followed (staffing, pay, training, performance, retention) and why.
  3. Effective HR Strategies (Evidence-Based)
    Identify at least 2–3 strategies that worked well. For each:
    1. What you did
    2. Evidence it worked (metrics/trends)
    3. Link to readings
  4. HR Investments That Paid Off
    Where you allocated resources well (e.g., compensation, training, hiring) and what outcomes improved.
  5. What You Would Do Differently
    Provide at least 2–3 improvements, supported by data and course concepts.
  6. Connection to Course Readings (Required)
    Connect results to at least 4 readings and key course frameworks (strategic alignment, HR analytics, HPWS, etc.).
  7. Team Process Reflection
    How your team coordinated, made decisions, and what collaboration lessons were learned.
