Welcome to HRM 830: HR Technology and People Analytics

This course introduces students to the core concepts, tools, and practical applications of Human Resource (HR) metrics and people analytics. The focus of the course is on using HR data to solve real-world organizational problems, support evidence-based decision-making, and enhance workforce and talent management strategies. Building on prior coursework in global human resource management, students will explore how HR analytics and HR technologies such as Human Resource Information Systems (HRIS), are used to collect, analyze, and interpret workforce data.

Throughout the course, students will engage in hands-on learning activities designed to develop both analytical and critical thinking skills. Weekly modules include readings, videos, simulations, and applied exercises that reinforce key concepts related to HR metrics, data analysis, and technology-enabled HR decision-making. Students will learn how to interpret complex HR data, assess the validity and reliability of analyses, and communicate findings in clear, plain language that can be understood by organizational leaders and stakeholders.

Major course assignments include short HR application papers, textbook-based case studies, business application activities using HR and statistical tools, and weekly simulation activities. These components culminate in a Predictive HR Analytics Project, where students will work with real or realistic workforce data using Excel or other statistical tools to analyze and predict outcomes such as employee turnover, performance, and workforce needs. This project allows students to integrate course concepts and demonstrate their ability to apply HR analytics in practical organizational contexts.

Active participation is expected each week through Canvas discussions, where students will share perspectives, connect course content to professional experiences, and engage in respectful, thoughtful dialogue with classmates and the instructor. Students are expected to post an initial discussion response by midweek and respond to peers by the end of each week.

To support student success, the course provides access to a required textbook, additional readings and multimedia resources, simulation tools, and university library resources. Students are encouraged to use these resources to deepen their understanding of course topics, support their written work, and develop the analytical skills necessary to succeed in modern, data-driven HR roles.
