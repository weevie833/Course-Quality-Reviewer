In order to effectively lead a team, the structure of the team should be well organized and thoughtfully planned. Whether the team is focused on a specific project, an organizational unit or department, it is critical that the manager has developed a comprehensive organizational plan. Depending on the maturity of the organization, you may need to plan its structure from the beginning, or address specific organizational issues. In both cases, you need to create a plan to address the situation.

For this assignment, students will develop an organizational plan (for a new structure) or an organizational change management plan (to address issues with an existing structure). They can utilize their capstone project team requirements or alternately choose a self-developed or researched case study.

The organizational plan should include, but not be limited to, elements such as:

  • Organization / team structure
  • Roles and responsibilities
  • Skill requirements and development activities
  • Team on boarding requirements
  • Resource allocation and acquisition planning
  • Succession and knowledge transition planning
  • Financial implications
  • Stakeholder expectations
  • Team dynamics strategies

The organizational change management plan should include, but not be limited to, elements such as:

  • Brief description of the mission for communicating the change strategy
  • Stakeholders
  • Overall goal of the implementation or changes to the organizational structure
  • Brief description of the current and future state with potential impacts
  • Communication Plan
  • Training Objectives if indicated
  • Information necessary to prepare for upcoming changes, i.e. policy/procedures
  • Training Required because of the changes
  • Post implementation steps
