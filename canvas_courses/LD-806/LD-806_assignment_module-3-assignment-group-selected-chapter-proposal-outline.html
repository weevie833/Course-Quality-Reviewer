Prior to this Assignment, each Group should have already agreed on, selected, and communicated the selection of the chapter contribution:

  • Fundraising vs. Development
  • Fundraising Strategies
  • Development Strategies
  • Identifying Prospective Donors
  • Building and Nurturing Relationships
  • Diversity and Inclusion
  • Ethical Considerations
  • Technology’s Role

Meeting via TEAMS, groups will lay out a proposal outline for their respective chapter that will include up to 5 sections (not including conclusion and references). References can be either from materials in class or available on the Internet (peer-reviewed sources).  All material, including references and resources, must be either under an open license, like Creative Commons, or in the Public Domain.

Proposal outline should include section titles with a one sentence explanation for what will be covered with another sentence about why it is relevant. Each section should correspond with a learning outcome (or more), which can be reverse engineered once a rough draft of the chapter is written. Please submit the outline by Sunday at 11:59 PM EST.

Basic outline can look something like this:

  1. Section 1 Title
    1. Explanation
  2. Section 2 Title
    1. Explanation
  3. Section 3 Title
    1. Explanation
  4. Section 4 Title
    1. Explanation
  5. Section 5 Title
    1. Explanation

Chapters should not exceed 2,500 words and/or 20 minutes worth of total video length.

Please create and submit 1-page proposal as Word Document that discusses the nature and structure of the Chapter Topic your group has selected.

Things your group should consider in crafting this proposal include the following:

  • The target audience for this OER. This OER will be written for nonprofit executive leadership, boards, or entry-level staff who may not yet have experience in – or necessarily understand the differences between – fundraising and development.
  • What are the proposed learning outcomes that will be met in the Chapter your group is contributing? These outcomes should be clearly stated at the beginning of the chapter to assist the reader in evaluating the chapter’s relevancy to what they want/need to learn.
  • How might fundraising and development differ for different types of nonprofit organizations? In this class, for instance, we make some distinctions between cultural and human service nonprofit organizations.
  • Additionally, please consider and add to your outline what methods will you use to teach to these outcomes in  your group's chapter. Will you record any videos? Can the interviews with the nonprofit executive directors and board members be included possibly as case studies?

Submission is due Sunday by 11:59 pm.
