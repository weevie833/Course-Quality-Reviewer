Peer review is invaluable for ensuring the quality of educational content. Its presence signals to a prospective adopter that the work has passed through rigorous quality control, and that its content is an accurate and reliable resource for others to use.

This is especially significant when working with OER, as the quality, comprehensiveness, clarity, and currency of open educational resources (OER) is often called into question by naysayers. OER, due to their low-cost nature and ease of creation/publication, are mistakenly perceived as low-quality. Peer review is important to dispel these notions, and to encourage wider use and adoption of the OER – which is ultimately the goal of most projects. 

External perception aside, peer review is fundamentally a means for you to receive valuable feedback on the OER content your group has worked on thus far, and make it stronger.

One of the last deliverables for our class OER project is to review one another's work to ensure that the content is appropriate, accurate, adequately covers the material, and is cohesive. 

One member per group will be the lead peer review coordinator in curating and organizing the peer reviews of other groups' chapters. What should be turned in is one document with a completed rubric for each reviewed chapter.

Please use the OER Rubric to guide your peer review.

How your group organizes and participates in the peer review is up to your group members - a few options might be:

  • Each group member reviews a different chapter, and completes the rubric for their assigned chapter independently;
  • Each group member reviews all chapters, and completes the rubric for all chapters independently;
  • Group members get together via TEAMS for a synchronous review session and complete the review together.

Please have the lead peer review coordinator submit their group's review document by Sunday at 11:59 PM.
