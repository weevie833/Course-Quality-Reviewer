Welcome to Fundraising and Resource Development!

In this class, you will not only learn about fundraising and resource development, but you will also have the unique opportunity to create and share learning content as part of an online educational resource (OER). This OER will be free and accessible to nonprofits or any organization who wants to take a measured and intentional approach to fundraising and resource development.

As you will learn, fundraising and resource development is not merely about asking for money or building/developing relationships, etc., although these are part of the process(es). Rather, fundraising and resource development contain many ‘pieces’ that – at the organizational level – entail many steps and involve numerous stakeholders, both internal and external.

As you work your way through the class...

Consider ways in which you could reframe and re-present course content in ways that a nonprofit or community-minded organization might find useful. This OER does not need to cover all the proverbial bases. Rather, think of this OER as an expandable resource that can be further developed by future classes.

Your assignments in each module will help you consider questions that you may feel should serve as the basis for this OER. What will this OER look like? This is a question you will answer, as you will decide as a class the structure of this OER as well as your contribution(s) to it.

Real-World Examples

Check out some real-world examples of open pedagogy in practice. Pay close attention to the Additional Ideas section, as it provides some ideas on ways to possibly structure the OER. 

As you complete your write-up each week and post it for discussion, consider how it can tie into an OER. Consider voice. While you may have a strong voice and want to demonstrate it, consider how your voices will coalesce into learning content that will be accessed by organizations. Several considerations here include tone, first-person vs. third, and audience.
