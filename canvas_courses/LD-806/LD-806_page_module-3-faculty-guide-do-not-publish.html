In this module, students explore the factors influencing donor values and perspectives on philanthropy, which are crucial for effective fundraising. Topics include corporate and foundation giving, demographic variations such as gender, ethnicity, age, and socioeconomic status, and the impact of LGBTQIA+ identities, generational differences, and high-net-worth donors on giving behaviors.

Module Objectives

By the end of this module, students should be able to:

  • Analyze the factors that influence donor values and perspectives on philanthropy.
  • Distinguish between corporate and foundation-giving practices.
  • Create an infographic, PPT, or other media that describes how an organization can reveal donor values and ways to appeal to those values.
  • Assess how demographic variations, including gender, ethnicity, age, and socioeconomic status, impact giving behaviors.
  • Explore the influence of LGBTQIA+ identities and generational differences on philanthropy.
  • Identify the motivations of high-net-worth donors and incorporate this understanding into fundraising strategies.

Conclusion

This module provides students with a comprehensive understanding of the diverse factors that shape donor values and perspectives on philanthropy. By analyzing different giving practices and demographic influences, students learn to develop effective fundraising strategies tailored to specific donor groups. Students also assemble content for their OER, ensuring their work aligns with the OER structure.
