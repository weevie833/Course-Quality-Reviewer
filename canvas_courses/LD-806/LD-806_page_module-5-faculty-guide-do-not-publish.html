In this week’s module, students explore the art and science of solicitation, focusing on effective methods for asking for donations, including events, direct mail, grant writing, corporate solicitation, and digital outreach. They learn how to tailor their fundraising strategies based on research and project needs. A 30-minute recorded interview with a fundraising professional offers insights into general approaches, structuring fundraising efforts, and the importance of stewardship in successful solicitation.

Module Objectives

By the end of this module, students should be able to:

  • Understand essential solicitation methods, including events, direct mail, grant writing, corporate solicitation, and digital outreach.
  • Tailor fundraising strategies based on donor research and specific project needs.
  • Analyze the importance of stewardship and its role in successful fundraising efforts.
  • Gain insights into professionally structuring and executing effective fundraising campaigns.
  • Create a strategic media piece (infographic, PPT, or other) that focuses on engaging and resonating with donors.
  • Adhere to APA guidelines while producing media content and provide constructive feedback to peers.

Conclusion

In this module, students explore effective solicitation techniques and the importance of aligning fundraising strategies with project needs and donor research. By analyzing insights from a professional interview and other sources, students should gain a deeper understanding of the art of the "ask" and the role of stewardship in building lasting donor relationships. This knowledge will be a foundation for students’ future fundraising efforts.
