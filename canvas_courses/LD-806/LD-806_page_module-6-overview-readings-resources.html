Overview

Effective fundraising and development must include a careful consideration of the laws and regulations that govern such activities, which include interactions with donors, staff, and volunteers. These ethical considerations include confidentiality of all privileged information, solicitation and use of funds, the presentation of information, and appropriate forms of compensation. 

However, these considerations do not just pertain to when a gift is secured and acknowledged. Rather, ethical practice and transparency includes reporting back to various stakeholders regarding the ‘return on investment’. If a gift was given for a particular program, for example, were the funds used for that purpose? What successes were achieved? What if there were unanticipated challenges? For grants, in particular, reporting back on investment is not only important, but also often part of a more formal stewardship process.

Aside from the nature of a gift and its acknowledgement, ethical practice and transparency relates to the actual handling of the money and all aspects related to it, including donor record maintenance, gift accounting, financial management and audit trails. In other words, it is important that the accounting side of receiving a gift aligns with what is promised by the fundraiser/development professional to donors/philanthropists.
