During this optional video session, the outline and nature of the OER project will be shared and discussed. Class contributions for the OER will consist of chapters (one per group plus an Introduction to Fundraising & Development). The structure of the individual chapters—video, text, etc.—can be decided by each group once they individually meet.

Things groups should consider in crafting their respective chapter proposals include the following:

  1. The target audience for this OER:
    • This OER will be written for nonprofit executive leadership, boards, or entry-level staff who may not yet have experience in – or necessarily understand the differences between – fundraising and development.
  2. What are the learning outcomes that will be met in the chapter?
    • These outcomes should be clearly stated at the beginning of the chapter to assist the reader in evaluating the chapter’s relevancy to what they want/need to learn. These outcomes can also guide you as you write the chapter.
  3. How might fundraising and development differ for different types of nonprofit organizations?
    • In this class, for instance, we make several distinctions between cultural and human service nonprofit organizations.

The title of the OER can be decided by the class as a whole through a discussion in an ensuing module. (This should be fun!)
