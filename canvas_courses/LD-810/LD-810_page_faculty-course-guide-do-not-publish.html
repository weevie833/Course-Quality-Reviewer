This graduate-level course provides a comprehensive understanding of the principles and practices necessary for assessing and managing change within organizations. Students will explore the essential communication tools required to implement change effectively, with a focus on client and vendor satisfaction, morale, and relationship management. The course emphasizes the importance of adaptability and strategic thinking to ensure that projects and organizational initiatives respond effectively to evolving conditions. Through this course, students will develop the skills needed to lead and manage change successfully in various organizational contexts.

Learning Outcomes

Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:

  1. Examine Interpersonal Skills for Effective Change Leadership

    • Understand the critical role of interpersonal skills in leading teams and project groups.
    • Explore how effective communication, empathy, and collaboration contribute to successful change initiatives.

  2. Analyze Theories of Organizational Communication

    • Study and apply selected theories of organizational communication.
    • Assess how these theories influence the effectiveness of change management when leading teams.

  3. Determine Solutions to Challenges in Change Dynamics

    • Identify common challenges that arise during the process of leading teams through change.
    • Develop strategic solutions to address these challenges and ensure smooth transitions.

  4. Apply Motivation Theory in Change Management

    • Explore various motivation theories and their application in managing change.
    • Implement motivational strategies to support and inspire team members during times of change.

Course Content and Structure

The course will cover the following key topics:

  1. Introduction to Change Management

    • Definition and importance of change management
    • Key concepts and principles of change management

  2. Theories of Organizational Communication

    • Application of communication theories to change management
    • Case studies and practical examples

  3. Managing Change Dynamics and Challenges

    • Identifying and addressing common challenges in change management
    • Techniques for managing resistance to change
    • Strategies for maintaining morale and relationships

Assessment Methods

  1. Participation and Engagement: Active involvement in discussions and group activities.
  2. Video Assignments: Creation and presentation of videos discussing various aspects of change leadership (change management plan)
  3. Simulations: An opportunity to practice change management and understanding the use of levers.
  4. Communication plan: this is assessed in Anthology and reviews student's competencies as well as grades. The competencies as on a 5 point scale. The competency score is in no way related to the student's academic grade.

    1. Students have been exposed to the skill/concept.

    2. Students demonstrate a basic understanding of the concept.

    3. Students demonstrate a moderate knowledge of concepts.

    4. Students demonstrate the ability to explain the concept to a novice (level 1 or 2).

    5. Students demonstrate their ability to teach the skill or concept to others.

  5. A course reflection paper: to highlight the student's learning.

Conclusion

This course is designed to equip students with the knowledge and skills necessary to become effective change leaders. Through a combination of theoretical understanding and practical application, students will be prepared to manage and lead change initiatives successfully in diverse organizational environments.
