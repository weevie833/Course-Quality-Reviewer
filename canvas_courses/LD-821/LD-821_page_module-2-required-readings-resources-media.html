Overview

This week we begin to frame our conversation on the basics of ethics and morality.  We will discuss how we conceive of, think about and determine what is "right" and "wrong".  This philosophical subject is really important to our everyday lives, as well as to leadership, as we are constantly confronting decisions that have ethical implications. 

To get you started, I would suggest that you take a look at this clip that I show in my on-ground classes.  While you view this speech by Nobel Peace Prize Winner Dr. Mohammad Yunus, I want you to think about what values motivated him to found the Grameen Bank and the impact that it has had on the country of Bangladesh.  I hope you enjoy his comments.

Morality is the term used to describe the constructs of human conduct and/or values.  At its base, morality is formulated on the understanding of what is preferred behavior, in an individual and societal sense.  It is often in the interaction of personal and societal factors that philosophers and thinkers contemplate the depth and uniqueness of this study.  

Though many theorists differ in their interpretation of where morality comes from or how personally or collectively it impacts us, experts are in general agreement that morality is a combination of reason and “sense”, that we as individuals employ or fall back on to determine right from wrong or our expectations of ourselves.  Using the writings of Plato in commentary on Socrates, the definition focuses on morality as the determination of “how we ought to live”.  

The definition of morality directly coincides with our beliefs about the future and how we conceive of the way the world, the people, and the factors that determine that world, should interact, and the end result we desire for ourselves, others, and the world around us.

Ethics is the formal study of both the personal and collective definitions of morality.  This philosophical study focuses on how we, individually or in groups, conceive or come to the determination of what morality is.  It also represents the constant reevaluation and thinking behind the decisions that have led us to such conclusions.  The term “ethics” comes from a derivative of the Greek term “ethos”.  This term was most closely connected to the Greek concept of ‘proper character or manners”.  

As a result, the definition of ethics, whether used as a discipline or as a concept, focuses on the pursuit of Truth in order to determine better decision outcomes on a daily basis for all involved, whatever the factors are and whatever the results.  Inherent in the study is an element of objectivity that is crucial to the overall success of the study.  Moral reasoning is the series of factors, arguments, and thinking patterns that humans use or engage in to determine what the basic values or constructs of proper moral judgments should be.  

The term moral reasoning focuses on why and/or how we as people achieve the end result of a proper way of living life.  Though this is obviously complicated, the fact remains that all of us engage in this reasoning on a daily basis and throughout our lives, whether we consciously know it or not.  The question really is:  can we improve our awareness of this reasoning?

All three of these terms are crucial to beginning to determine the Truth of a situation.  It is inclusive of how to more carefully and diligently read individuals who one will interact with.  By paying more close attention to these constructs and studying them in greater depth, we can be more understanding of the factors that determine better decisions and of course, in turn, avoid the prospect of decisions that could be very costly.
