Module Overview

This module introduces students to the ideologies of objective and subjective thought, providing a foundation for exploring the question of what constitutes a "good life." Through discussions and training, students will examine how these ideologies influence ethical perspectives and decision-making. Additionally, students will complete the UNH IRB (Institutional Review Board) training to understand ethical considerations in research involving human subjects. Insights from this module can be integrated into their signature assignment in Module 7.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, students should be able to:

  • Differentiate between objective and subjective thought and understand how these perspectives influence ethical decision-making.
  • Reflect on and articulate their understanding of what constitutes a "good life."
  • Recognize the ethical implications of research practices through the completion of UNH IRB training.
  • Integrate insights from this exploration into their philosophy of leadership ethics, to be articulated in their signature assignment.

Conclusion

This module encourages students to engage deeply with the concepts of objective and subjective thought, using these frameworks to explore fundamental ethical questions about the good life. Through discussion and formal IRB training, students will develop a nuanced understanding of how these ideologies influence ethical decision-making and research practices, laying the groundwork for their signature assignment in Module 7.
