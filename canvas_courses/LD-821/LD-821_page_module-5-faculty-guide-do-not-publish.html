Module Overview

This module focuses on helping students understand the various factors that contribute to unethical practices within organizations. Students will engage in a discussion about the factors they believe are most responsible for unethical decisions and participate in a case study discussion related to institutional research training. The insights gained from these activities will contribute to their signature assignment in Module 7.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, students should be able to:

  • Identify and analyze key factors that contribute to unethical practices in organizations.
  • Critically assess the most significant factor that leads to organizational misjudgments.
  • Engage in discussions that deepen their understanding of how institutional research can support ethical decision-making.
  • Apply their insights from the discussion and case study to their developing philosophy of leadership ethics, which will be reflected in their signature assignment.

Conclusion

This module encourages students to critically examine the factors that contribute to unethical practices in organizations, providing them with the tools and insights necessary to develop a robust philosophy of leadership ethics. Through discussions, case study analysis, and reflective exercises, students will gain a deeper understanding of the challenges and responsibilities involved in leading ethically within organizations.
