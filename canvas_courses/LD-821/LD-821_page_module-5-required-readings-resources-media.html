Overview

It is important to consider what factors contribute to unethical practices in organizations.  As you read through chapter five and seven, make sure to pay particular attention to the case studies. 

As you consider this, make sure to think about how problems in organizations reflect practices that are more systemic.  Based upon how frustrating it can be to think about this and the prevalence of unethical thinking and behavior in organizations, it is easy to fall into a framework which acknowledges that we have little or no power to change factors that influence us or our society. 

In a certain sense, this belief permeates our society and in reality is only somewhat true.  We are a product of factors around us such as our background, our environment, our education, our thinking, and our beliefs.  The issue is whether we have any means in which to work beyond such factors if they, in fact, are leading us to make decisions that either continue potentially destructive habits. 

Will we simply follow in the path that has been directed for us?  What is the role of the leader in influencing others in this regard?  The answers to these questions are complicated but there must be some acknowledgement that free will must be tempered with determinism, and not be left to the extreme viewpoint of fatalism or cynicism as we consider the value of leadership and its influence. 

Choices do matter whether you think them to be determined by other factors or not.  We are left with assessing, to the best of our ability, the conceptions and knowledge given to us, with the understanding that whether we conceive of determinism or free will, we still must progress into the future.  Coming to grips with the struggle of making good decisions in our lives is centered on ethical leadership built on our thoughts and actions.

Objectives:

  1. Identify factors that contribute to unethical practices in organizations
  2. Formulate possible solutions to those problems
  3. Consider ethical leadership as more than just organizational or managerial responsibility
