Overview

In conclusion, it is important to consider the values that support the successful enacting of the attributes of a critical thinker as it directly connects to building leadership in community, small or large. Good critical thinking found in ethical study is based upon the development of respectful, fair and just treatment found in human thinking patterns. If we want to be wiser it is imperative that we work towards the values listed above so that we operate in an ethical manner regardless of the situation and/or the outcomes.

At the core of these ethical end goals, is the important characteristic of civility. In a world that often is unfair, unjust, and disrespectful, it is imperative as thinkers that we keep in mind how important good communication based in a strong conception of civility is, in order to promote the values discussed above.

In order to be civil so that we can promote understanding and good communication, communications (not philosophers—the connection to our lives today) experts Gudykunst and Kim have seven principles of civility that are important to our study of critical thinking and the practicality of philosophical dialogue but also very important overall to ethical relationship building.

One, we must be committed with our attitude, our willingness to enact change, and our sacrifice of time to this process.

Two, we must be aware of others and decrease our “assuming” inclinations. The experts refer to this as “mindfulness”.

Three, we must be committed to acceptance, even when we disagree. Disagreement does not mean that we must defeat those who disagree with us. Instead, we should be promoting resolutions that minimize hurt and damage to others and to ourselves.

Four, we need to be openly and honestly concerned for the plight of others. This should be a lifestyle change that starts with our thinking and not a simple acknowledgement that is soon forgotten.

Five, we must be willing to listen and understand.

Sixth, we must be ethical and hold ourselves accountable when we violate what we know is the right or truthful path.

And last, we must advocate through understanding and enhance communication and dialogue, good thinking that supports enhanced constructive citizenry and helps build healthy and productive societies where people, both as individuals and as groups prosper and live meaningful and rewarding lives.
