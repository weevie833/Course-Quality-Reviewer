Course Overview

This course is designed to explore the foundational principles and practical applications of ethics in leadership. Students will engage with various ethical frameworks, historical perspectives, and contemporary issues, all aimed at developing a robust understanding of what it means to be an ethical leader. The course culminates in a signature assignment, an ethical treatise, where students articulate their philosophy of leadership ethics. This treatise is scaffolded throughout the course, with each module building toward the final submission.

Module 1:

In the first module, students will begin their exploration of ethics by considering the foundational aspects of thought and practice that contribute to the development of an ethical framework and the creation of ethical leaders. This module sets the stage for the course by introducing students to key Greek thinkers and leaders whose ideas have significantly influenced Western ethical thought. Students will engage in activities that encourage self-reflection and lay the groundwork for their signature assignment.

Module Objectives

By the end of this module, students should be able to:

  • Understand the contributions of Greek thinkers to the development of ethical frameworks.
  • Begin to reflect on their career paths and the ethical decisions they have made.
  • Identify how these reflections and historical insights may inform their philosophy of leadership ethics.
  • Create an infographic to introduce themselves and connect with their peers.

Conclusion

This first module is designed to introduce students to the foundational concepts of ethics and morality through the lens of Greek thinkers and leaders. By reflecting on their own careers and ethical decisions, students will begin to develop a personal philosophy of leadership ethics, which will be expanded upon throughout the course. The activities in this module are intended to build a strong foundation for the signature assignment, the ethical treatise, which will be due in Module 7.
