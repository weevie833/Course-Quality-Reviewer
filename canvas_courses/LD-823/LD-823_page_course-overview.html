Welcome to Emergence of a Strategic Leader!

In this course, you will explore strategic leadership from the leadership perspective. We will emphasize theoretical foundations, practical applications, and contemporary issues in the field. You will learn decision-making strategies, engage with stakeholders, plan for human resource needs (and succession planning), critically assess diversity and inclusion, motivation strategies, information management strategies, tactics for overseeing operations and administration, and manage social and ethical issues.

Each week, we will discuss module topics in forums and flip grids. We will practice our presentation skills. We will create an infographic to highlight important human resources strategies. Over the term, you will develop a strategic leadership plan; there are three assignments in the course that contribute to the plan. Lastly, you will identify aspects of this course you will apply to your capstone and reflect on the elements of this course you will use in your workplace.

In each of the courses in the program, it will be important that you are well acquainted with the Graduate Resources and Orientation. This page provides numerous resources and important information to help you on your journey here at CPS Online.

The Course Resources page in each of your courses will also be essential to your success in this program. It provides resources, tutorials, and tips needed to complete many assignments. Sections are divided into Rubrics (specific to each course assignment), Assignments (specific instructions for each major course assignment), Technology, Writing & Information Literacy, and MS Leadership Capstone (if applicable) resource sections. Should you ever be looking for resources, this is where you will locate them.

Make sure to note that there are several APA 7th Edition resources available in the Writing & Information Literacy section. Most recently we have added an OWL Purdue APA Discussion Referencing Guide to support you while participating in online discussions.

As we work to build a more inclusive classroom culture, we must recognize the importance of honoring each student’s identity and expression within their course experience.  If your chosen name differs from the class roster or your pronouns change at any point throughout the term, please email me separately and I will work to update the class roster/records to ensure you are seen and honored for your true self throughout the semester.
