Overview

This module will cover strategic human resource management and continue the Strategic Leadership Plan assignment with the Literature Review section.

Strategic leaders wear many hats, and one of the most crucial is that of a human resource manager. Their decisions shape the entire team, from selecting the right individuals to guiding their development. Boards of Directors, for example, hold immense power in appointing, evaluating, and even dismissing the CEO – a decision that has a ripple effect throughout the organization.

Compensation also falls under the strategic leader's purview. Setting salaries for top executives can influence their behavior and the organization's overall performance. Different incentive structures and pay disparities can have a significant impact. However, the strategic leader's role in human resources goes beyond simple compensation. Research suggests that CEOs prioritizing strategic human resource management systems – training programs, performance reviews, and fostering a positive work environment – can significantly impact the company's success.

This area offers fertile ground for future research. How exactly do strategic leaders leverage HR to their advantage? Can they use rewards and appointments to motivate strategy execution and better performance, or might these incentives lead to intense competition or unethical behavior? Understanding the nuances of a strategic leader's role in human resource management is key to unlocking an organization's full potential.
