Module Overview

In this final module, students will explore the complexities of managing social and ethical issues and navigating conflicting demands in strategic leadership. The module serves as a reflective culmination of the course, encouraging students to integrate their learning and consider how they will apply their knowledge to future leadership challenges, including their upcoming capstone project. Students will participate in two discussion forums and complete a reflective essay based on what they have learned from the course. This final reflection helps students solidify key concepts and prepares them to approach strategic leadership with a deeper understanding of ethical decision-making and conflict management.

Module Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, students will:

  1. Reflect on their learnings from the course, focusing on how they can apply strategic leadership principles to manage social and ethical issues in organizations.
  2. Analyze how the course has shaped their approach to managing conflicting demands and how they can use this knowledge in their upcoming capstone project.
  3. Synthesize key course concepts in a reflective essay, highlighting personal and professional growth in strategic leadership.

Instructor Role:

  • Encourage students to reflect deeply on the social and ethical dimensions of leadership they encountered throughout the course. Guide them in thinking about how these concepts are theoretical and practical in real-world leadership.
  • Provide examples from the course materials or real-world cases to help students draw connections between ethical decision-making and leadership.
  • Monitor the discussion to ensure students are engaging with one another’s reflections in a meaningful way.
  • Encourage students to think practically about how the course’s lessons can be applied in their capstone project, focusing on managing ethical dilemmas and conflicting demands.
  • Guide students in identifying specific frameworks or leadership models from the course that can be applied to their capstone.
  • Monitor the discussion to ensure students are collaboratively and supportively engaging with one another’s approaches to the capstone.
  • Encourage students to reflect deeply on their learning journey, emphasizing both personal and professional growth in strategic leadership.
  • Guide on structuring a reflective essay, helping students connect specific course concepts to their experiences and future leadership roles.
  • Offer feedback on how students can integrate theoretical insights with practical applications in their leadership practice.

Conclusion

In this final module, students will reflect on the key learnings from the course and consider how they will apply these insights to future leadership challenges, including their capstone project. The discussion forums provide opportunities for students to engage in meaningful reflection and share strategies for integrating course concepts into their professional practice. The reflective essay allows students to synthesize their learning, highlighting their growth as strategic leaders. By the end of this module, students will be prepared to take on the complex challenges of social and ethical leadership and manage conflicting demands with confidence and strategic insight.
