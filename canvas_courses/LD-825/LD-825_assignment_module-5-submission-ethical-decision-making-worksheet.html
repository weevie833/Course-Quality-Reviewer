You are the director of volunteers for a non-profit. The organization received an anonymous phone call reporting that an unnamed student was accusing one of your volunteers, Jake, of stealing $50 while working at the Christmas Tree fundraiser. Jake is a court ordered community service volunteer, who was found guilty of underaged drinking, he has 20 hours to complete and the alleged theft took place during his second shift. The staff member that took the call reported she knew the volunteer had a “reputation as a troublemaker.”  You are aware that the staff member’s son and the volunteer had recently had an in incident at school.  The staff member disagreed with your decision to allow the person to complete community service hours with your non-profit.

What are the ethical dilemmas in this situation?

Is your responsibility to the volunteer, the staff member, the non-profit, or your professional code of conduct?

Consider these questions as you complete the ethical decision-making worksheet.
