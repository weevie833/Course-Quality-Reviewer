Consider everything you have experienced in this course and up to this point in your program.  Connect your course work, real world experiences, professional standards and what you have learned from your colleagues in this 3-5 page narrative using APA style. Use your readings to ground your reflection.  For example, you may want to cover:

  • What are the key lessons learned from this course?  From your program up to this point?
  • Reflect on the learning outcomes of this course and their importance to the overall program.
  • Describe your successes and how you achieved them.
  • Describe your challenges and what you learned from them?
  • Describe how you have grown professionally as related to the programmatic standards.
  • Reflect and describe how the skills learned from this course were or can be applied in your professional life.
  • Describe your next goals in your professional development.  Use the standards as a guideline.  

This assignment is designed to assist you in developing your Capstone e-Portfolio.  The process of reflecting on the past, present and future, is creating documentation of your professional growth over time which will be used in your final project.  You will be asked to integrate and build on your prior course work and experiences and demonstrate your professional skills through a Capstone e-Portfolio which you will create and present at the end of your program.

How will your Narrative Assignment be Assessed and Graded?

Here are the assessment criteria:

Those that are listed below are objective criteria. For example, the use of proper citations is a criterion. So, make sure that when you are writing your Narrative, that you use outside sources. You should cite those sources properly. Professors will use this assessment tool to measure progress and to provide you periodic feedback on your demonstrated professional growth in these competency areas. The competency assessment does not impact your grade in the course and you are being assessed against a standard of “exemplary” that only highly experienced and skilled professionals should expect to meet.  If you have questions about the feedback you are receiving in your competency assessments, you should contact your faculty member or program director to discuss these concerns.

Assessed Criteria

  • Knowledge: Articulates key concepts, theories, and practices
  • Connections: Makes connections between professional experience, course work, and standards.
  • Self-Awareness: Sees self-awareness as a practitioner in the field.
  • Evidence: Uses evidence to support the analysis or explanation.
  • Sources: Incorporates outside sources and evidence.
  • Introduction: Constructs an effective introductory paragraph
  • Transitions: Incorporates fluid transitions
  • Sequence: Incorporates a logical sequence of ideas and themes.
  • Conclusion: Ends narrative with a clear summary and/or conclusion.
  • Fluency: Fluency of the writing.
  • Word Choice: Uses precise word choices and fluent, economical phrasing.
  • Grammar: Uses vocabulary, grammar, and spelling according to standard written English
  • Structure: Effectively uses standard English conventions.
  • Citations: Proper use of citations
