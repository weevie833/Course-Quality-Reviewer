Module Overview

In this first module, students will be introduced to key terminology, resources, and trends in volunteer leadership. This foundational module sets the stage for the rest of the course by providing students with the context and vocabulary they need to engage in more complex discussions about volunteer leadership in subsequent modules. Students will also explore significant contributions made by volunteers and volunteer programs, reflecting on the broader impact of volunteerism in various sectors.

The video discussion forum is a key component of this module, where students will introduce themselves, share their experiences with volunteerism, and begin to build a sense of community within the course.

Module Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, students will:

  1. Understand and use key terminology related to volunteer leadership and management.
  2. Identify and explore important resources that support volunteer programs and leadership development.
  3. Discuss current volunteerism trends and reflect on volunteers' significant contributions in various sectors.
  4. Introduce themselves and share their past volunteer experiences, setting the foundation for peer interaction and collaboration throughout the course.

Instructor Role:

  • Encourage students to share detailed, personal reflections on their volunteer experiences, emphasizing how these experiences shaped their approach to leadership.
  • Provide a welcoming and supportive environment in the discussion forum, offering feedback on student posts and encouraging peer engagement.
  • Monitor the discussion to ensure students actively engage with one another and build a sense of community.
  • Provide clear explanations of key terminology and ensure students understand how these terms will be used throughout the course.
  • Offer students curated resources that they can explore independently to deepen their understanding of volunteer management practices.
  • Encourage students to reflect on the trends they are most interested in and how these trends align with their goals as future volunteer leaders.
  • Encourage students to reflect on the societal value of volunteerism, emphasizing the broad impact volunteers have in different fields.
  • Provide examples of significant volunteer contributions in various sectors, helping students understand the scope and importance of volunteer leadership.
  • Monitor the discussion to ensure students are providing thoughtful, reflective responses and engaging with one another.

Conclusion

In this first module, students will be introduced to the foundational terminology, resources, and trends in volunteer leadership, setting the stage for the rest of the course. The video discussion forum will allow students to introduce themselves and share their past volunteer experiences, while the discussion on significant contributions helps students reflect on the broad societal impact of volunteerism. By the end of this module, students will have established a strong understanding of the course’s key concepts and begun building a sense of community with their peers, creating a foundation for more in-depth learning in future modules.
