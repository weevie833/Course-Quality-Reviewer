Module Overview

In Module 2, students will explore the importance of volunteer engagement, creating tool kits for managing volunteers, conducting needs assessments, and developing service descriptions for volunteer roles. This module helps students understand how to effectively engage volunteers and define clear roles and expectations for their service. The module includes two discussion forums: one focused on the value of understanding volunteers and the other on creating service descriptions. The service description assignment will play a key role in the paper students will write in Module 3 and the Volunteer Handbook due in Module 7.

Module Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, students will:

  1. Understand the importance of knowing and engaging volunteers to maximize their impact and satisfaction.
  2. Develop a service description for a volunteer role, defining clear expectations and requirements.
  3. Begin incorporating knowledge from this module into their paper for Module 3 and the Volunteer Handbook due in Module 7.
  4. Apply volunteer management tools such as tool kits, needs assessments, and service descriptions to enhance the structure and effectiveness of volunteer programs.

Instructor Role:

  • Encourage students to think about volunteer engagement as a two-way relationship, where volunteers and leaders benefit from understanding one another’s motivations and strengths.
  • Provide examples of tools and strategies for getting to know volunteers better, such as volunteer profiles, regular check-ins, or needs assessments.
  • Monitor the discussion for meaningful engagement and ensure students are offering constructive feedback to one another.
  • Guide students in developing clear and comprehensive service descriptions, emphasizing the importance of defining volunteers' roles, expectations, and responsibilities.
  • Provide examples of compelling service descriptions, helping students understand how these tools contribute to volunteer engagement and organizational success.
  • Monitor the discussion to ensure students provide thoughtful feedback and improve their service descriptions based on peer input.

Faculty Guide for Volunteer Leadership Course (Master’s Level) – Module 2: Volunteer Engagement, Tool Kits & Needs Assessments, and Service Descriptions

The module includes two discussion forums: one focused on the value of understanding volunteers and the other on creating service descriptions. Module Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, students will:

  1. Understand the importance of knowing and engaging volunteers to maximize their impact and satisfaction.
  2. Develop a service description for a volunteer role, defining clear expectations and requirements.
  3. Begin incorporating knowledge from this module into their paper for Module 3 and the Volunteer Handbook due in Module 7.

Module Schedule

Week 2: Discussion Forum – The Value of Knowing Your Volunteers

Objective: Students will discuss the importance of understanding volunteers’ motivations, skills, and interests, and how this knowledge contributes to volunteer engagement and retention.

  • Discussion Prompt:

    • Reflect on the value of knowing your volunteers. How does understanding their motivations, skills, and interests impact their engagement, satisfaction, and long-term retention in a volunteer program?
    • Share an example (real or hypothetical) of how knowing volunteers personally helped or could help improve their performance and satisfaction within a volunteer role.
    • How can volunteer leaders create opportunities to get to know their volunteers better? Discuss specific strategies or tools (e.g., surveys, one-on-one meetings, volunteer profiles) that help leaders engage more meaningfully with volunteers.

  • Instructions for Students:

    • Initial Post: Write a 250-300 word post discussing the value of knowing your volunteers and how this knowledge influences volunteer engagement, performance, and retention.
    • Response Posts: Respond to at least two peers (150-200 words each), offering feedback on their strategies and providing additional suggestions for engaging volunteers through personal connection.

  • Instructor Role:

    • Encourage students to think about volunteer engagement as a two-way relationship, where both volunteers and leaders benefit from understanding one another’s motivations and strengths.
    • Provide examples of tools and strategies for getting to know volunteers better, such as volunteer profiles, regular check-ins, or needs assessments.
    • Monitor the discussion for meaningful engagement and ensure students are offering constructive feedback to one another.

  • Evaluation Criteria:

    • Depth of reflection on the value of knowing volunteers and how it impacts engagement.
    • Quality of examples provided to illustrate how knowing volunteers improves performance and satisfaction.
    • Constructive feedback and additional insights offered in response to peers’ posts.

Week 2: Discussion Forum – Creating a Service Description

Objective: Students will create a service description for a volunteer role, clearly outlining the role’s responsibilities, expectations, and requirements. This service description will contribute to the Volunteer Handbook due in Module 7.

  • Discussion Prompt:

    • Based on your past experiences (either real or hypothetical), create a service description for a volunteer role within an organization.
    • Your service description should include the following elements:
      1. Role Title: Clearly define the volunteer’s role.
      2. Responsibilities: Outline the key duties and tasks associated with the role.
      3. Qualifications: Specify any skills, experience, or qualifications required for the role.
      4. Time Commitment: Provide details on the expected time commitment, including hours per week and the duration of the role.
      5. Benefits: Highlight any benefits volunteers can expect, such as professional development, networking opportunities, or recognition.
    • In your post, explain why it’s important to have clear service descriptions for volunteers. How do these descriptions contribute to volunteer satisfaction, engagement, and performance?

  • Instructions for Students:

    • Initial Post: Write a 250-300 word post that includes a service description for a volunteer role and a reflection on the importance of service descriptions in volunteer programs.
    • Response Posts: Respond to at least two peers (150-200 words each), providing feedback on their service descriptions and suggesting ways to improve clarity or add additional details.

  • Instructor Role:

    • Guide students in developing clear and comprehensive service descriptions, emphasizing the importance of defining roles, expectations, and responsibilities for volunteers.
    • Provide examples of effective service descriptions, helping students understand how these tools contribute to volunteer engagement and organizational success.
    • Monitor the discussion to ensure students are providing thoughtful feedback and improving their service descriptions based on peer input.

  • Evaluation Criteria:

    • Completeness and clarity of the service description, including all required elements.
    • Reflection on the importance of service descriptions in volunteer engagement and performance.
    • Quality of feedback provided to peers, including constructive suggestions for improving service descriptions.

Week 2: Resource – Preparing for the Volunteer Handbook (Module 7)

Objective: Students will begin preparing content for their Volunteer Handbook, focusing on creating a strong foundation with service descriptions, tool kits, and needs assessments.

  • Resource Overview:

    • In preparation for the Volunteer Handbook, students should start developing content related to service descriptions, tool kits, and needs assessments.

    • Volunteer Handbook Sections to Develop:

      1. Service Descriptions: Begin collecting or refining service descriptions for key volunteer roles. These descriptions should include clear expectations, responsibilities, qualifications, and time commitments.
      2. Tool Kits: Consider what resources and tools volunteer leaders can provide to support volunteers in their roles. These may include instructional guides, communication protocols, or job aids.
      3. Needs Assessments: Reflect on the needs of the organization and the volunteers. How will you assess volunteer needs (e.g., through surveys, interviews, or evaluations) and ensure that volunteers are placed in roles that match their skills and interests?

    • Relation to Future Assignments:

      • The service description developed in this module will form part of the Volunteer Handbook due in Module 7.
      • This service description will also be a key component of the paper students will write in Module 3, where they will explore how volunteer roles are defined and aligned with organizational needs.

  • Instructor Role:

    • Encourage students to actively use the content from this module to begin developing their Volunteer Handbook.
    • Provide feedback on service descriptions, needs assessments, and tool kits, ensuring students understand how to apply these concepts to real-world volunteer management.
    • Remind students that this is an ongoing process, and the material developed in this module will contribute to their signature assignment in Module 7.

  • Evaluation Criteria:

    • N/A (This is a preparatory resource to guide students toward completing their Volunteer Handbook in future modules).

Additional Resources

  • Service Description Templates: Provide students with sample service description templates to guide their development of clear and structured volunteer role descriptions.

  • Needs Assessment Tools: Share resources or articles on conducting needs assessments, emphasizing how these assessments can be used to match volunteers with appropriate roles and ensure organizational needs are met.

  • Volunteer Engagement Strategies: Offer resources that outline best practices for engaging and retaining volunteers, focusing on strategies that involve understanding their motivations and providing clear role expectations.

Conclusion

In Module 2, students will focus on the importance of volunteer engagement and creating service descriptions to manage volunteers effectively. The discussion forums challenge students to reflect on the value of knowing their volunteers and creating clear service descriptions. Additionally, students will begin preparing content for their Volunteer Handbook, which is due in Module 7. These activities will help students develop the tools necessary to engage, support, and manage volunteers successfully, building a foundation for future assignments and leadership roles in volunteer management.
