Module Overview

In Module 3, students will explore strategies related to recruitment, with a focus on inclusion and diversity in volunteer programs. This module emphasizes the importance of creating diverse and inclusive volunteer teams and understanding the modern tools used for recruitment, particularly online platforms. Students will engage in a video discussion forum where they will create a recruitment video, a second discussion forum focused on the benefits of diversity in organizations, and a paper analyzing online recruitment platforms for volunteer programs.

Module Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, students will:

  1. Understand the importance of recruitment strategies that promote inclusion and diversity within volunteer programs.
  2. Develop and present a video demonstrating effective volunteer recruitment techniques.
  3. Reflect on the benefits of diversity in volunteer organizations and how diverse teams contribute to organizational success.
  4. Analyze online recruitment platforms and their effectiveness in reaching and engaging diverse volunteer candidates.

Instructor Role:

  • Encourage students to think about the recruitment process from a marketing and engagement perspective. Guide them to craft messages that speak to a wide audience, using inclusive language and visuals.
  • Provide examples of effective recruitment videos that are engaging and appeal to a diverse audience.
  • Monitor the video forum for thoughtful feedback, ensuring students are constructively critiquing one another’s recruitment messages.
  • Encourage students to explore the multifaceted benefits of diversity, including innovation, problem-solving, and increased empathy within teams.
  • Provide examples of organizations successfully leveraging diversity to improve program outcomes, helping students understand how inclusive volunteer teams contribute to organizational success.
  • Guide students in reflecting on the challenges of managing diverse teams and offer strategies for overcoming these challenges.
  • Provide guidance on how to research and analyze online recruitment practices, offering examples of popular tools used in volunteer recruitment.
  • Encourage students to think critically about the role of digital tools in engaging volunteers, particularly in reaching underrepresented or diverse groups.
  • Offer feedback on how students can connect recruitment strategies with broader organizational goals related to diversity and inclusion.

Conclusion

In this module, students will explore the critical topics of recruitment, inclusion, and diversity in volunteer leadership. The video discussion forum encourages students to develop practical recruitment messages, while the discussion forum on diversity helps students reflect on the value of diverse teams. The paper on online recruitment platforms challenges students to analyze modern tools for reaching and engaging volunteers, with a focus on promoting diversity and inclusion. By the end of this module, students will have a deeper understanding of how to craft recruitment strategies that are inclusive, diverse, and aligned with organizational goals.
