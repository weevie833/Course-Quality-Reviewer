Module Overview

In Module 4, students will focus on key volunteer management processes: screening, interviewing, orientation, and training. These foundational elements ensure that the right volunteers are selected, well-prepared, and aligned with the organization’s mission. The module includes a video discussion forum where students will describe screening questions for potential volunteers, allowing them to engage in a peer review of best practices in volunteer selection. Additionally, students are introduced to resources that will support the development of their Volunteer Handbook, which is due in Module 7.

Module Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, students will:

  1. Understand the importance of effective screening and interviewing processes in selecting volunteers who align with the organization’s values and needs.
  2. Develop effective screening questions and interviewing techniques that assess volunteer suitability.
  3. Reflect on the components of a successful orientation and training program for volunteers.
  4. Begin incorporating the knowledge from this module into the Volunteer Handbook assignment due in Module 7.

Instructor Role:

  • Encourage students to think critically about the qualities and characteristics essential for volunteers in their program. Guide them to create screening questions that assess skills, motivation, cultural fit, and commitment.
  • Provide examples of practical screening questions that assess key volunteer attributes (e.g., reliability, teamwork, adaptability).
  • Monitor the discussion to ensure students engage thoughtfully with one another’s video posts and provide constructive feedback.
  • Encourage students to actively incorporate the knowledge from this module into developing their Volunteer Handbook.
  • Remind students that the Volunteer Handbook is due in Module 7, and this module is an essential step in building the necessary content.
  • Encourage students to reflect on their experiences with orientation and training in volunteer settings and how those experiences influence their approach to volunteer management.
  • Guide students to think about the unique needs of volunteers, including their skill levels, motivations, and learning preferences, and how these factors can shape an effective orientation and training program.
  • Provide examples of successful orientation and training programs and emphasize how these elements contribute to long-term volunteer retention and satisfaction.

Conclusion

In this module, students will delve into the critical processes of screening, interviewing, orientation, and training in volunteer leadership. The video discussion forum encourages students to share and evaluate screening questions for potential volunteers. At the same time, the Volunteer Handbook preparation activity helps students begin incorporating these concepts into their final assignment. By reflecting on their experiences with orientation and training, students will also consider how to create effective volunteer programs that engage, support, and retain volunteers. This module provides essential skills for selecting and preparing volunteers to contribute meaningfully to organizational success.
