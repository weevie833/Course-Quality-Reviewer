In Module 6, students focus on appreciation and recognition within volunteer leadership. This module emphasizes the importance of expressing gratitude toward volunteers, especially considering generational differences and virtual environments. Students will engage in a discussion forum on the value of expressing gratitude and complete an interview assignment that explores critical aspects of volunteer leadership, including recruitment, training, appreciation, and retention. The module encourages students to think critically about how volunteer appreciation contributes to volunteer satisfaction and retention across different generations and in various organizational settings.

Module Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, students will:

  1. Understand the importance of expressing gratitude and recognizing volunteers, with a focus on generational preferences and virtual environments.
  2. Analyze how appreciation impacts volunteer satisfaction and retention, particularly in a leadership context.
  3. Interview a volunteer leader to gain insights into recruitment, training, appreciation, and retention practices, and write a reflective paper based on the interview.
  4. Apply course concepts to real-world volunteer leadership challenges, focusing on effective appreciation and recognition strategies.

Instructor Role:

  • Encourage students to think critically about how expressing gratitude impacts volunteer retention and motivation, especially with volunteers from different generational backgrounds.
  • Provide examples of effective gratitude and recognition strategies in both in-person and virtual volunteer settings.
  • Guide students in considering the unique challenges and opportunities of expressing gratitude in remote and virtual environments.
  • Guide students in preparing for and conducting their interviews by offering examples of relevant questions and strategies for engaging volunteer leaders in meaningful conversations.
  • Ensure students understand how to structure their reflection paper, connecting the interview insights to course concepts on recruitment, training, appreciation, and retention.
  • Offer support to students who may experience technical difficulties accessing the assignment instructions, advising them to download the file if necessary.

Conclusion

In Module 6, students will explore the crucial role of appreciation and recognition in volunteer leadership. The discussion forum encourages students to reflect on how generational differences and virtual environments influence the way gratitude is expressed, while the interview assignment allows students to gain real-world insights into volunteer leadership practices related to recruitment, training, appreciation, and retention. These activities help students apply course concepts to real-world challenges, ensuring they develop the skills needed to effectively lead and appreciate diverse volunteer groups. By the end of this module, students will have a deeper understanding of how expressing gratitude can enhance volunteer engagement and retention, particularly in today’s multigenerational and increasingly virtual environments.
