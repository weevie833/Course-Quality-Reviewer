Module Overview

In this module, students will focus on generational volunteering and the role of core values in volunteer leadership. These topics help students synthesize their understanding of managing diverse volunteer groups and aligning volunteer programs with the organization’s values. The module includes two discussion forums: one on generational differences in volunteer organizations and the other on core values in leadership. Students will also complete their signature assignment, which involves creating a comprehensive Volunteer Handbook. This handbook will integrate concepts throughout the course, focusing on effectively managing volunteer programs across generations and aligning them with core organizational values.

Module Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, students will:

  1. Analyze generational differences in volunteer organizations and how these differences impact volunteer management and engagement.
  2. Reflect on the role of core values in leadership and how they influence volunteer programs.
  3. Create a comprehensive Volunteer Handbook incorporating course concepts such as volunteer recruitment, management, retention, and core values.
  4. Demonstrate the ability to apply course concepts to real-world volunteer leadership challenges, particularly in managing generational diversity and aligning programs with organizational values.

Instructor Role:

  • Encourage students to think critically about the differences between generational groups and how those differences manifest in volunteer settings.
  • Provide guidance on strategies for managing and engaging multigenerational teams, such as tailored communication, flexible volunteer roles, and varied recognition methods.
  • Monitor the discussion for insightful engagement and challenge students to consider how generational differences influence their leadership approach.
  • Guide students in identifying their personal leadership values and help them understand how those values influence their decision-making and leadership style.
  • Facilitate discussion on the challenges and benefits of aligning organizational values with those of volunteers, encouraging students to think about how values influence engagement and retention.
  • Monitor the discussion to ensure thoughtful engagement and encourage students to apply real-world examples to their analysis.
  • Ensure students understand the importance of the Volunteer Handbook as a leadership tool that brings together the major concepts covered throughout the course.
  • Provide feedback on the handbook’s structure, content, and practicality, focusing on how well students integrate the course concepts into a cohesive document.
  • Offer support to students who may have difficulty accessing the assignment instructions, advising them to download the file if needed.

Conclusion

In this module, students will bring together everything they’ve learned throughout the course, focusing on generational volunteering and core values in leadership. The discussion forums provide an opportunity for reflection on these key topics. At the same time, the Volunteer Handbook serves as the signature assignment, challenging students to create a practical, comprehensive guide for managing volunteers. This module encourages students to think critically about how to lead diverse volunteer groups while staying true to the organization's values. By the end of the module, students will be equipped with the knowledge and tools to become effective and value-driven volunteer leaders.
