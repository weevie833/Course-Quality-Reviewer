This course is one of several graduate courses that qualify for a badge, also referred to as a digital badge.

The badge has already been set up in your course so, as the instructor, you do not need to set this up.  If the student completes this course with a B- (80%) or better, a badge will be issued automatically to the student 11 days after the end of the course (3 days after final grades are due).  Students will be notified via their UNH email.  You can also see who has received the badge via the Credentials link in the course navigation.

IMPORTANT NOTE ABOUT GRADES

The Total score, visible in the last column of the Canvas gradebook, does not factor in ungraded items.  However, the Final Score does factor in ungraded items. If an assignment is not graded, the value is calculated as 0 which may lower the Final Score significantly to a non-passing grade.  The badge in your course is automatically issued based on the Final Score, not the Total score.  Because of this, it is critical that you change any empty grade to "Excused" if the student is not required to submit that assignment.  This is particularly true for an eportolio assignment that is required for specific degree majors.  See Change the Status of a Submission in the Gradebook to learn how to change a grade to "Excused". 

What is a Badge (Micro-Credential)

A digital badge represents a skill, achievement, or experience. The college defines and evaluates skills and/or competencies that are represented in the badge. Sharing badges is learner-controlled and open across multiple technology platforms including social media, blogs, online portfolios, etc. The predefined learning paths allow students to earn badges and stacked badges (micro-credentials) that can lead to a certificate or degree depending on the path the students choose. . To learn more, see the UNH Micro-credentials website.

At the College of Professional Studies (CPS), there are several Graduate Learning Pathways. To read more about these pathways see the Learning Pathways with UNH CPS section of the Micro-credentials website.  As of Fall 2024, UNH is now using the Canvas Credentials badging platform (formerly known as Badger). This system replaced Credly Badges.
