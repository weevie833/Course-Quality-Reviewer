In Module number 1 students will express opinions about career preparation for nonprofit work. Should preparation for a career in the nonprofit sector emphasize management skills or a broader understanding of civil society and the role of nonprofit organizations? Students will introduce themselves by creating an infographic which can be a helpful tool for future presentations. Module one will also begin the process of reviewing the legalities associated with nonprofits.

Why It’s Important

Who are we? In this opening module, we will get to know each other and hopefully begin to build trust. We will become kind and encouraging in our dialogue, enhancing our learning.

Sarbanes-Oxley Act: Students will learn about this most important law that governs nonprofits' tax deductibility. It describes specific reporting requirements, etc. As the term progresses, we will review more regulatory issues. A complex system of laws and oversight at the federal, state, and local levels regulates nonprofits. This regulation ensures nonprofits operate in the public interest and protect the public from fraud.

Stakeholders: Throughout the term, students will identify stakeholders and how we can engage them in the mission. In week one, students will give some examples of stakeholders from their perspective. Nonprofit Stakeholders refer to any individual or group that has an interest in your nonprofit. They include people directly involved, such as board members, beneficiaries, donors, or foundations that give you grants, and those indirectly involved, such as the media or the local community.
