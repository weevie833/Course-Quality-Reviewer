In Module 2, students will learn how nonprofit organizations depend on leadership to provide guidance internally and externally. Governing Boards provide many crucial functions, including legal responsibility for expenditures and partnerships, vision and mission alignment, and the experience and resources each board member brings to the organization. The role of the Governing Board has changed as expectations about ethics and accountability for nonprofit organizations have increased.

Why It’s Important

A Broad Scope: In the early stages of investigating nonprofits, students must understand functions and legal requirements for reporting to funding sources and the government. Almost all charitable nonprofits recognized as tax-exempt by the IRS must file an annual report with the IRS, known as the “Form 990.” IRS Form 990 is a public document available on GuideStar and from charitable nonprofits upon request under IRS “public disclosure” regulations.

Diversity and Inclusion: Most nonprofits are community-based and serve diverse populations. When assembling a board, the community being served must be included. It is important to have organizational values published on the nonprofit’s website or otherwise shared publicly.

Leadership: Some essential attributes of a nonprofit leader include empathy and self-awareness. Empathy helps leaders understand others' values, motivations, and feelings, which can help them align their values with the team's goals. Self-awareness is crucial because it involves being aware of personal strengths and abilities.
