In Module 3, students will become familiar with and be exposed to various performance indicators and measures.

Students will select an organization they are affiliated with (volunteering, a faith-based organization, sports clubs, causes, etc.) or an organization they have researched. They shall give examples of this organization's inputs and outcomes. What activities does the nonprofit engage in, and what are the intended outcomes of those activities? There will be a practical task of selecting a nonprofit for which they will conduct a program evaluation for their signature assignment.

Why It’s Important

Planning: Many tools and assessments are used in the planning and evaluation process. Students will learn about a variety of tools and techniques for both planning and evaluation. “If you can measure it, you can manage it.”

Stakeholder involvement: To successfully plan for nonprofits, stakeholders must be consulted and included. Nonprofits have a variety of stakeholders – that is, individuals and groups with a stake in their work. This includes people directly involved, such as board members, beneficiaries, donors, or foundations, and those indirectly involved, such as the media or local community. Without them, a nonprofit will fail.

Topic Selection: The final assignment is to create a program evaluation for an agency that interests students and with whom they are very familiar, such as where they work. Students must select a program and briefly describe it. Consider some aspects we've discussed, such as the board selection and stakeholder discussion.
