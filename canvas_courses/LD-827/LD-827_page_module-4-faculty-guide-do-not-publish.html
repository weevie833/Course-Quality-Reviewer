This module seriously examines Strategic Planning and program analysis. Students will become familiar with Logic Models. A logic model is an organized and visual way to display one's understanding of the relationships among the resources you have to operate your program, the activities you plan, and the changes or results you hope to achieve.

The module will also engage students in the importance of solid evaluation teams. Evaluation teams conduct assessments of the performance, skills, and overall quality of nonprofit programs. These teams should include stakeholders and staff at all levels.

Why It’s Important

  • Assuring Success: In addition to gaining and sustaining external support, the evaluation provides the data, analysis, and strategies for your leadership and staff to improve, refine, expand, reduce, or create new programs and services. It lets your board and staff know whether goals are being met and how to improve and do even better.
  • Creating a Visual Description of Your Path: The logic model Brings detail to broad goals and helps in planning, evaluation, implementation, and communications. Helps to identify gaps in our program logic and clarifies assumptions so success may be more likely. It builds understanding and promotes consensus about the program and how it will work. Builds buy-in and teamwork
  • Purpose of a Balanced Scorecard: A balanced scorecard is a strategic planning framework that companies use to assign priority to their products, projects, and services, communicate about their targets or goals, and plan their routine activities. The scorecard enables agencies to monitor and measure the success of their strategies to determine how well they have performed.
