This module focuses on collaborations and partnerships. What makes partnerships successful, and what makes them go awry? Students will discuss what they have observed in nonprofit organizations that have entered partnerships. What factors made the work well, and what factors contributed to failure? Students will also view the pros and cons of collaborations by putting themselves in the position of a director of a nonprofit that another has approached to establish one. What data and factors would they examine to make a realistic decision? Would it make a difference if it was a government entity?

Why It’s Important

  • Leveraging resources:  Understanding how partnerships might be able to fill in resources in a way that supports both agencies.  Will collaborations help a nonprofit or tax its ability to achieve its goals?
  • Pros and Cons of Nonprofit Collaboration: Students must understand the benefits and possible pitfalls of entering formal collaborative agreements. They will also become aware of the concept of “Strategic Philanthropy.”
  • Internal and external analyses: Students will conduct SWOT and PEST analyses to determine the agency's needs and whether partnerships are necessary.
