Overview

Welcome to Week 2!

Where do social conflicts come from? They are often rooted in four main components - the identity we share with a group, a sense of grievance, a desire to make things better and the hope that change is possible. This makes social conflicts complex.

Interpersonal level conflicts often begin because of a lack of communication and trust. Over the next few weeks, you will have the opportunity to try several tools that help to overcome these barriers to understanding and alignment.

This week you will explore these components as well as your own personal way of working through conflict. You will have the opportunity to explore this by taking an assessment and learning about the different types of conflict management styles people use. Although we typically have one style that we gravitate to, it is helpful to understand the different styles as what is most successful is looking at the situation and applying a style that would best fit the circumstances.
