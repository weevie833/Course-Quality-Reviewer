Getting Past No

This Module focuses on the five-step process to “Get Past No,” as described by William Ury. It also focuses on determining the BATNA for each conflict, which is the Best Alternative To A Negotiated Agreement.

The Five Steps are explained as:

  • Step 1: Don’t React (Go to the Balcony)
  • Step 2: Disarm Them (step to their side)
  • Step 3: Change the Game (don’t reject… reframe)
  • Step 4: Build the Golden Bridge (make it easy to say yes)
  • Step 5: Don’t Escalate – Use Power to Educate (make it hard to say no)

Learning Objectives

By the end of this Module, students shall:

  • Be able to explain the BATNA Concept
  • Be able to apply the Five-step process to get to yes.

Conclusion and learning outcomes

Students will be able to understand and apply the multi-dimensional processes of conflict resolution theories, principles, and methodologies to real-world domains relevant to their emphasis of study, such as family, business, and education.
