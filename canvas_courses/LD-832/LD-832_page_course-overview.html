Welcome to Building Diverse & Inclusive Organization!

Successful leaders must learn to integrate different viewpoints to enhance creative problem solving, leadership ability and organizational effectiveness. This course will provide an overview of cultural diversity and socialization in today’s organizations. Students will be exposed to key concepts, models and issues of diversity in the organization and global society. The ethical implications of managing people with various social identities will be examined.

In each of the courses in the program it will be important that you are well acquainted with the Graduate Resources and Orientation. This page provides numerous resources and important information to help you on your journey here at CPS Online.

The Course Resources page in each of your courses will also be essential to your success in this program. It provides resources, tutorials, and tips needed to complete many assignments. Sections are divided into Rubrics (specific to each course assignments), Assignments (specific instructions for each major course assignments), Technology, Writing & Information Literacy, and MS Leadership Capstone (if applicable) resource sections. Should you ever be looking for resources, this is where you will locate them.

Make sure to note that there are several APA 7th Edition resources available in the Writing & Information Literacy section. Most recently we have added a OWL Purdue APA Discussion Referencing Guide to support you while participating in online discussions.

As we work to build a more inclusive classroom culture, we must recognize the importance of honoring each student’s identity and expression within their course experience.  If your chosen name differs from the class roster or your pronouns change at any point throughout the term, please email me separately and I will work to update the class roster/records to ensure your seen and honored for your true self throughout the semester.
