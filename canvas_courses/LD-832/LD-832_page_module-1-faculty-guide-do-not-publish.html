Module Overview

This module introduces students to demographics as a foundational aspect of diversity and inclusion. By examining the role of demographics in shaping workplace and societal dynamics, students will begin to understand how identity categories such as race, gender, age, and socioeconomic status impact the experiences of individuals and groups. The module also serves as an introduction to the course, allowing students to introduce themselves and reflect on the topics of diversity and inclusion most relevant to their personal and professional interests.

Module Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, students will:

  1. Develop an understanding of demographics' role in shaping diversity and inclusion efforts.
  2. Reflect on their identity and background, considering how personal demographics influence their experiences.
  3. Identify two topics in the course that are most interesting to them and explain why they resonate.
  4. Engage in meaningful discussions about how demographic factors influence diversity and inclusion in various settings.

instructor Role:

  • Encourage students to reflect on their own demographic identity with openness and thoughtfulness, emphasizing that this is a space for shared learning and respectful engagement.
  • Model an introductory post yourself to help students understand the tone and depth expected.
  • Foster a welcoming environment for diverse experiences and encourage students to engage meaningfully with their peers’ backgrounds and reflections.
  • Guide the conversation by helping students think critically about the importance of demographic data while also considering the potential challenges (e.g., stereotyping or overlooking individual differences).
  • Encourage students to draw connections between demographic factors and real-world diversity and inclusion initiatives they may have encountered in their personal or professional lives.
  • Ensure respectful and thoughtful engagement, especially when discussing sensitive or personal experiences related to demographic identity.
  • Provide students with clear instructions for reviewing the syllabus and selecting their topics of interest.
  • Encourage students to think deeply about why these topics matter to them and how they can apply what they learn to their personal and professional lives.
  • Offer feedback that helps students connect their selected topics to broader course themes and real-world DEI applications.

Conclusion

This module provides a foundation for understanding demographics and their influence on diversity and inclusion efforts. By introducing themselves and reflecting on their demographics, students will engage with the course on a personal level. At the same time, the discussions and reflection assignments will help them think critically about the role of demographics in shaping inclusion initiatives. Identifying two-course topics of interest will also allow students to tailor their learning experience to their personal and professional goals in diversity and inclusion.
