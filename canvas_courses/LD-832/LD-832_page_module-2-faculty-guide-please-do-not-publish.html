Module Overview

This module focuses on identifying and overcoming bias, emphasizing recognizing how biases manifest in the workplace and individual behaviors. Students will deepen their understanding of explicit and implicit bias through readings, discussions, and personal reflection. They will also take the Implicit Association Test (IAT) to reflect on their unconscious biases and how they can influence workplace dynamics.

Module Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, students will:

  1. Identify different types of bias in the workplace and understand their impact on diversity and inclusion efforts.
  2. Reflect on default behaviors and how they may perpetuate bias, drawing insights from the course book.
  3. Examine their biases through the Implicit Association Test and reflect on the results.
  4. Propose strategies to recognize, confront, and overcome bias in professional settings.

Instructor Role:

  • Encourage students to think broadly about how different forms of bias (e.g., gender, racial, age-related, ableist) appear in workplaces and the systemic factors that enable them.
  • Guide the conversation toward concrete strategies for recognizing and mitigating organizational bias.
  • Monitor the discussion to ensure respectful and constructive engagement.
  • Encourage students to engage in self-reflection, guiding them to connect personal insights to the concepts from the course book.
  • Facilitate a discussion that helps students become more aware of how deeply ingrained biases affect workplace behaviors and decision-making.
  • Provide feedback that emphasizes both self-awareness and practical ways to address bias.
  • Offer guidance on interpreting IAT results and managing any discomfort or surprise that students may experience.
  • Emphasize that the IAT is a tool for self-awareness, not judgment and that the reflection should focus on growth and learning.

Conclusion

This module encourages students to critically examine the role of bias in workplace dynamics, focusing on both explicit and implicit bias. Through discussions, personal reflections, and the Implicit Association Test, students will gain self-awareness and practical strategies for recognizing and overcoming bias in their professional environments. The module concludes with opportunities for synthesis and application, helping students connect theoretical insights to real-world workplace practices.
