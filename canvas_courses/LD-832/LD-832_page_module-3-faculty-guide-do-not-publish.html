Module Overview

This module examines the barriers to inclusion in various contexts, focusing on systemic oppression, workplace culture, and state and federal policies related to diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI). Through a case study discussion, students will analyze how workplace culture either promotes or inhibits inclusion. They will also engage in a discussion on systemic oppression, drawing connections between broader societal patterns and individual workplace practices. Additionally, students will submit a paper that evaluates state or federal DEI policies and standards, and they will have the opportunity to test their presentation skills by creating a professional video recording.

Module Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, students will:

  1. Understand the role of systemic oppression in creating barriers to inclusion in workplaces and society.
  2. Analyze the impact of workplace culture on diversity and inclusion through a case study.
  3. Examine state and federal policies related to DEI, identifying strengths and areas for improvement.
  4. Practice professional communication skills by recording a video presentation on DEI-related topics.

Instructor Role:

  • Guide students to critically reflect on systemic oppression beyond the individual level, encouraging them to think about institutional and cultural forces.
  • Encourage students to provide specific examples from research or case studies to ground their discussions.
  • Ensure respectful and constructive engagement, as the discussion may involve sensitive topics.
  • Facilitate the discussion by prompting students to think critically about the connection between workplace culture and DEI outcomes.
  • Encourage students to propose realistic and actionable strategies for fostering a more inclusive culture.
  • Ensure the discussion remains focused on practical applications and workplace examples.
  • Provide clear guidelines for selecting appropriate policies and examples for analysis.
  • If necessary, offer feedback on students’ paper outlines or drafts to ensure they are critically engaging with the policies.

Conclusion

This module encourages students to critically engage with workplace barriers to inclusion by examining systemic oppression, workplace culture, and DEI-related policies. Through discussions, case studies, and a professional video presentation, students will understand the challenges to fostering inclusion and how organizations and governments can improve DEI outcomes.
