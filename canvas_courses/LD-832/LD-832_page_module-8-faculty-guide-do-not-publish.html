Module Overview

In this final module of the Diversity course, students will submit their reflective essays, which serve as a critical reflection on their learning throughout the course. In addition, students will participate in a discussion forum where they will share their final takeaways from the course. This module encourages students to synthesize their learning, reflect on their personal growth, and engage in meaningful dialogue with their peers.

Module Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, students will:

  1. Submit a reflective essay demonstrating critical thinking about diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI) issues covered in the course.
  2. Reflect on and articulate their personal growth and key diversity-related insights.
  3. Engage in a thoughtful discussion with their peers about the most significant takeaways from the course.
  4. Connect their learning to future personal, academic, or professional contexts.

Module Wrap-Up and Key Takeaways

  • Instructor Reflection: Consider writing a final post or short video summarizing key course themes and encouraging students to continue applying the concepts they've learned beyond the classroom.

  • Encourage Future Engagement:

    • Suggest ways for students to stay engaged with DEI issues in their communities, workplaces, and academic environments.
    • Recommend additional readings or resources for students who want to explore DEI topics further.

Conclusion

This module allows students to reflect deeply on their learning journey and engage in meaningful discussions with their peers. By submitting their reflective essays and participating in the discussion forum, students will consolidate their understanding of diversity, equity, and inclusion while articulating how they plan to apply these concepts moving forward. The module’s emphasis on personal growth, peer engagement, and course content synthesis ensures that students leave the course with a strong foundation in DEI principles.
