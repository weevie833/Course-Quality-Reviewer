Here re the guidelines for Discussion participation: 

  • Original responses should be 350 to 550 words in length.
  • Original responses should be posted not later than THURSDAY for each module (the exception is the last week of the course, which has an earlier due date). Late posts are subject to a reduction in the grade.
  • Engage in discussions on at least 3 different days during the week.
  • Respond substantially to a minimum of two peers’ responses by no later than SUNDAY for each module (the exception is the last week of the course, which has an earlier due date.) Late posts are subject to a reduction in the grade.
  • Respond to instructor questions, as the discussion board is where a majority of our shared learning takes place.
  • Deliver information in your posts that show that thought, insight, and analysis have taken place.
  • Be timely in postings and responses so others have the time to read and respond and engage with you.
    • Remember to maintain an appropriate level of respect and professionalism when posting responses.
    • Feel free to share your own personal experiences to augment or supplement the point(s) you are making.
    • Make sure the answer adds substantially to the discussion.
    • Provide the appropriate links and/or citation where appropriate.
    • Be collaborative, not combative.
    • Be positive in approaching the subject matter.

Avoid the following responses:

  • Simple “I agree” or “Good point” statements alone.
  • Off-topic postings. Use the Virtual Student Lounge social forum for non-course-related discussion postings or additional postings. Use of the Virtual Student Lounge forum is encouraged and contributes to the fun!
  • Anything that could be interpreted as offensive by a fellow classmate.
  • Avoid off-color humor and language; at all times maintain courtesy and respect towards the other members of the class.
