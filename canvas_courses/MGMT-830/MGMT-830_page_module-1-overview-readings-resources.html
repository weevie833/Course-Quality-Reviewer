Overview

Strategic planning is a crucial process for organizations to set a clear direction, allocate resources effectively, and achieve long-term success. In the context of Information Technology (IT), strategic planning plays a pivotal role in ensuring that IT investments and initiatives are aligned with the overall business objectives. This week will delve into the importance of strategic planning in IT, the strategic planning process itself, and how to effectively align IT strategy with business objectives.

In our first week of this eight-week course, we will explore the critical role of strategic planning in today's rapidly evolving technological landscape. Participants will gain a comprehensive understanding of the challenges organizations face in aligning IT capabilities with business objectives and the importance of bridging the gap between these two key elements. Topics covered will include:

  • The Need for Strategic Planning: Examining the reasons why strategic planning is essential for organizations to remain competitive and achieve their goals in a technology-driven world.
  • The Gap Between IT and Business: Identifying the common challenges organizations face in aligning IT capabilities with business requirements and the potential consequences of misalignment.
  • Components of Strategic Planning: Exploring the various stages of the strategic planning process, including vision and mission development, SWOT analysis, goal setting, and strategy formulation.
  • Aligning IT with Business Objectives: Discussing the importance of collaboration between IT and business leaders in ensuring that IT investments deliver tangible value to the organization.

By the end of this week, students will have a solid foundation in strategic planning and understand the critical role it plays in driving organizational success in today's digital age.
