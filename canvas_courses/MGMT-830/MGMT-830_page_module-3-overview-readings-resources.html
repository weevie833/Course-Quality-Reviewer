Overview

In Module 3, we will delve into the fundamental concepts and terminology of financial management as applied to IT professionals. The focus will be on equipping participants with a solid understanding of key financial metrics and their relevance in managing IT projects and resources within an organization. Topics covered will include:

  • Financial Basics: A review of essential financial terms and concepts, such as revenue, expenses, profit, assets, liabilities, and equity.
  • IT Budgeting: The process of creating and managing IT budgets, including identifying cost drivers, allocating resources, and tracking expenditures.
  • Cost-Benefit Analysis: Evaluating the financial viability of IT projects by comparing potential benefits to associated costs.
  • Return on Investment (ROI): Calculating the profitability of IT investments and assessing their impact on organizational performance.
  • Financial Forecasting: Using historical data and future projections to anticipate financial trends and make informed decisions.

By the end of this week, students will be able to understand and integrate basic financial concepts and how these concepts work in a synergistic manner in the strategy and financial ecosystem of IT and IT project management.
