Overview

In Module 4 we will delve into the critical topic of IT investment decision making. Participants will gain a comprehensive understanding of the tools and techniques used to evaluate the financial viability of IT projects. Key concepts covered will include:

  • Capital Budgeting: The process of planning and controlling long-term investments in IT projects.
  • Net Present Value (NPV): A method for determining the present value of future cash flows associated with an IT investment.
  • Internal Rate of Return (IRR): The discount rate at which the NPV of an IT project becomes zero.
  • Payback Period: The time required for an IT investment to recoup its initial cost.
  • IT Investment Appraisals: The process of assessing the potential benefits, risks, and costs of IT investments.

By the end of this week, students will be equipped with the knowledge and skills to make informed decisions about IT investments and allocate resources effectively within their organizations.
