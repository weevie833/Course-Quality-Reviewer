Overview

Many organizations find it beneficial to have two distinct management paths; one focused on day-to-day operations, the other focused on those unique initiatives that are critical to achieving success with strategic objectives. The operations manager is usually a permanent position. Sometimes an operations manager will assume the role of project manager, but not change their official position title. A project manager’s “assignment” ends when the project is completed. That is the major difference between the roles. These roles might look totally different from one another, but, as you will discover this week, they have quite a bit of similarity. They need to be supportive of one another to succeed.
