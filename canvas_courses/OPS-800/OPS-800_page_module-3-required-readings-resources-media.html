Overview

In this module, you will explore the functions of supply chain management, through the lens of budget management. Careful balancing of these two functions often means the difference between success or failure to deliver, especially in a time of crisis. Imagine you are the operations manager overseeing a supply chain that has been disrupted by a political or military activity within the region from which you procure raw materials. 

Sourcing these materials from another geographical region could have significant impact on your budget, as well as the cost of goods you are producing. If you knew in advance the region was becoming unstable, you might have been able to negotiate with another provider before the crisis is on your hands. That is active supply chain management. You can learn more on this topic by enrolling in SCM 805, Supply Chain Management. 

As you read the "2021 Manufacturing Outlook" report (below), please think about how the discussed changes might be implemented within organizations. Each of these "transformational changes" represents a number of projects to be undertaken. Consider how project management processes to which you were exposed in PM800 might be applied to enhance the probability of a successful transformation.
