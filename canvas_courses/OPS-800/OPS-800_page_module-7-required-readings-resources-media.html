Overview

You are now ready to understand how the project management functions integrate with the operations management functions. In an organization that uses projects to accomplish the successful completion of a project, that is, a temporary and unique endeavor that has a defined beginning and ending dates, with specific deliverables, you might find operations managers assume the role of a project manager on a temporary basis, in addition to their normal responsibilities. In other organizations, the two management roles are distinct roles that support one another in the achievement of business objectives. Project management is often called “operations management in action” with the focus on only fulfilling the project scope. 

A common process in which the inputs from both managers is desirable is the development of a risk management plan. A project has risks that are related to, or the same as, an operational risk being monitored by the operations manager. A determination of the risk response in this case should address the exposure of the project and the organization. Another area of collaboration is in the determination of what to do when a supply chain, upon which a project is dependent, is unexpectedly disrupted. Not only does that disruption impact the operations, it also impacts the project. The project manager might experience difficulties with a preferred vendor delivering sub-optimal components. The relationship with that preferred vendor will most likely exists within the organization’s procurement or finance operations. In this scenario, the project manager and the vendor relationship manager would need to collaborate on a suitable solution.
