Overview

In the first part of this module, we will explore project management in a matrixed organization. A matrixed organization is one that recognizes the importance of vertical functional departments and the importance of horizontal process-focused teams. These teams will bring together staff from the pertinent functional departments to work together under a designated (project or product) manager, or team leader, to accomplish a specific objective within a specified time frame. Recall a project manager, a temporary assignment, has no permanent resources; the operations departments, permanent structures, “own” the resources. The assignment of staff from departments such as finance, human resources, purchasing, production to a product or project manager is often a point where operations managers need to collaborate with this manager, to ensure the project being led is adequately supported. In this module, you will analyze what it takes to effect smooth operations within these matrixed environments. You will explore the conditions under which the matrix is the best solution for an organization, and where it will not succeed. 

The second part of this module focuses on the criticality of communications within a matrixed organization. Not just for you the manager, but also for the staff. Staff will be operating in a dual manager environment – reporting to the operations manager for some activities, and to the project manager for others. You will analyze different reporting mechanisms that can clarify and make these communications less confusing for all involved. You will be exposed to the power of dashboards and key performance indicators, as well as regularly scheduled review meetings. Techniques to minimize the outward communications will be shared, along with some tools to support effective communications.
