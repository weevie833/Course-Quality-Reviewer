Overview

Risk planning should be initiated early in the planning stage of the project. It may be useful to invite subject matter experts and stakeholders such as customers or regulators to these initial sessions.  The risk management plan is a component of the overall project management plan and it defines how the project risk management activities will be structured and executed.

At the end of this module you should be able to:

  1. Describe the risk management process.
  2. Differentiate between the types of risks that can affect the outcome of a project.
  3. Illustrate approaches for risk identification, analysis, and assessment.
  4. Suggest approaches for responding to project risks and opportunities.
  5. Perform qualitative and quantitative risk analysis.
  6. Plan the appropriate risk strategies for a project according to threats, opportunities, and overall project risk.
  7. Differentiate the meaning and function of contingency reserves and management reserves in the context of a project.
