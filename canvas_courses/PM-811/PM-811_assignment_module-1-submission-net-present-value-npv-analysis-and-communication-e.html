As a Project Manager, understanding and applying financial analysis techniques such as Net Present Value (NPV) is critical when making informed decisions on project selection, especially in capital-constrained environments.

You are part of a project selection team evaluating two proposed projects. The company's accountants estimate that the cost of capital will remain stable at 2.5% for the next five years. Below is the cash flow information for each project. Your team must decide which project to select based solely on financial criteria.

Using your knowledge of the Time Value of Money (TVM) and Net Present Value (NPV), perform the following tasks:

  1. NPV Calculation:
    • Calculate the NPV for each project using MS Excel based on the provided cash flow information and the company's cost of capital (2.5%).
    • Ensure that your calculations include inflows and outflows for each project over five years.
  2. Financial Recommendation:
    • Based on your NPV calculations, which project would you recommend selecting? Justify your recommendation with a clear explanation of your financial analysis.
    • Consider other financial factors that might impact your decision (e.g., risk, the initial investment size, etc.).
  3. Communication Strategy:
    • Explain how you would communicate your recommendation to the organization's technical and non-technical stakeholders.
    • Discuss how you would present complex financial data, such as NPV results, to stakeholders with varying levels of financial expertise.
    • Provide strategies for ensuring your key points are clearly understood and any potential concerns or questions are addressed.
