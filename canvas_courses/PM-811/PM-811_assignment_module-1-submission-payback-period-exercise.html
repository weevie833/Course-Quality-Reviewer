Understanding the Time Value of Money (TVM) is essential for Project Managers, particularly when managing complex, multi-year projects with limited financial resources. In your role as a Project Manager, you are often faced with making critical decisions regarding the allocation of funds to projects that may impact the long-term financial health of your organization.

Consider the following scenario:

Two new projects have been proposed for a division of a corporation that is currently experiencing cash flow issues due to a downturn in its industry.

  • Project A has an estimated initial cost of $350,000 with projected annual returns of $50,000.
  • Project B has an estimated initial cost of $500,000 with projected annual returns of $100,000.

Both projects are expected to run for a similar duration, and all other factors, including risk and project timelines, are the same.

Using the concepts of Time Value of Money (e.g., Net Present Value, Discount Rate, and Internal Rate of Return), technical tools, and communication strategies you've learned in this course, answer the following:

  1. Which project would be the best choice from a financial perspective? Justify your answer using TVM calculations (e.g., NPV, IRR). You may use a reasonable discount rate based on your research.
  2. In addition to financial analysis, how would you communicate your recommendation to stakeholders with varying levels of financial literacy? Explain how you would adapt your communication style for technical and non-technical audiences.
  3. What are the potential risks or limitations of selecting either project and how would you address them in your final recommendation?
