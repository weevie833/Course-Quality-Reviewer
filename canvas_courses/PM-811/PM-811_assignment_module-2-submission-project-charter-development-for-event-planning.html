As a Project Manager, you ensure that projects are initiated with a well-defined scope, clear objectives, and a structured plan. This assignment challenges you to apply advanced project management principles, integrating strategic analysis, technical precision, and leadership accountability into the planning process.

Scenario:

You have been tasked with planning a significant event, such as a family gathering, or an office party for your colleagues. The event is expected to host at least 30 attendees, and you will take on the role of the Project Manager. As the first step in managing this project, you must develop a comprehensive Project Charter that outlines the critical components necessary for successful planning and execution.

Assignment Requirements:

Using the concepts of project initiation, your Project Charter must demonstrate advanced integration and technical understanding by including the following components:

  1. Project Goal and Objectives:
    • Define the event's overall objective using the SMART framework (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, and Time-bound). Ensure that your goals align with the needs of the event's stakeholders.
  2. Scope and Key Deliverables:
    • Clearly outline the deliverables required for the event's success, such as venue selection, catering, entertainment, and attendee management.
    • Define the project scope with specific boundaries and exclusions to ensure no expectation ambiguity.
  3. Requirements and Stakeholder Engagement:
    • Identify the event's essential requirements, including logistical needs, compliance with organizational policies (if applicable), and guest preferences.
    • Provide a detailed analysis of the stakeholders involved in the project (attendees, vendors, colleagues, etc.) and discuss how you plan to manage stakeholder communication and expectations.
  4. Assumptions, Constraints, and Dependencies:
    • Outline the assumptions made during the planning process and the constraints (e.g., budget, venue limitations, time restrictions).
    • Discuss any dependencies between project tasks or external factors that could impact the event's success.
  5. Risk Identification and Mitigation:
    • Identify three significant risks affecting the event's success (e.g., vendor delays, budget overruns, venue availability).
    • Develop a risk management plan that includes mitigation strategies for each identified risk. Demonstrate your understanding of risk analysis by assessing the likelihood and impact of each risk.
  6. Preliminary Schedule:
    • Using your knowledge of project scheduling techniques, develop a high-level project timeline or Gantt chart that shows the major milestones and tasks required to complete the project. Ensure that the schedule reflects realistic time frames for each phase of the event planning process.
  7. Preliminary Budget:
    • Provide an estimated cost breakdown for the event, including key categories such as venue rental, food and beverages, entertainment, and other costs. Discuss budgetary constraints and your approach to maintaining financial accountability throughout the project.
  8. Sponsor Approval and Accountability:
    • Your instructor will act as the project sponsor for this project. As the Project Manager, you must ensure that the Project Charter is thorough and accurate before presenting it for approval.
    • Develop a strategy for ensuring sponsor engagement and accountability, addressing how you will maintain communication with the sponsor throughout the project's lifecycle to ensure continued alignment with the event's objectives.

Deliverable Format:

  • The Project Charter must be submitted in Microsoft Word format, using Times New Roman, 12-point font, with double spacing.
  • You may use any project charter template available through the Course Resources or one of your choice. However, ensure that your charter reflects advanced integration of project management principles, including technical and strategic components.

Submission Guidelines:

  • In addition to the Project Charter, submit a one-page reflection detailing your approach to creating the charter, discussing how you incorporated advanced project management techniques, stakeholder management strategies, and financial oversight.
  • Be prepared to discuss your Project Charter and reflect on potential challenges, risks, or assumptions in class.
