Consider the following situation: There is a major system outage reported at a telecommunications company. The issue was caused by the company that developed the software for this system. 

A software engineer had been authorized (using the formal change process) to correct a defect.  While he had the code in his control, he noticed another defect and fixed that while he was there.  He tested the software on the equipment in the test lab and replaced the software in the configuration management tool. 

When their customer installed the upgrade, the system crashed taking out dial tone for 100,000 households. Sometimes a member of the project team can incorporate a change without communicating that change to anyone else on the project.  While disasters do not always happen, is it ever a good idea to allow this to happen?  How can it be prevented?
