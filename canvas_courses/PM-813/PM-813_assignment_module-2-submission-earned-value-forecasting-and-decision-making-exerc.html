Scenario:

You are managing a critical project to build a prototype passive sonar system for the US Navy. The project is set to be completed within one year. Success in this phase could secure a much larger contract worth over five billion dollars, which involves installing systems at eight sites worldwide and providing several years of services and equipment. However, this long-term opportunity hinges on the success of the current contract. You are preparing for the first quarterly internal review with the vice president of your organization, where you must present an analysis of the project's current performance and propose any necessary corrective actions.

The total budget for the project is $200 million, and you have allocated $180 million of this budget across tasks, as seen in the table below. Your role is to evaluate the project using Earned Value Management (EVM), assess its current status, and make informed decisions on how to proceed.

Instructions:

  1. EVM Calculations:
    Based on the data provided, calculate the following critical metrics for the project so far:
    • Earned Value (EV)
    • Planned Value (PV)
    • Actual Cost (AC)
    • Cost Variance (CV)
    • Schedule Variance (SV)
    • Cost Performance Index (CPI)
    • Schedule Performance Index (SPI)
    • Estimate at Completion (EAC)
    • Variance at Completion (VAC)
  2. Percent Complete and Percent Spent:
    • Calculate the overall percentage completed for the project.
    • Calculate the overall Percentage of Budget Spent (relative to the total $200 million budget).
  3. Interpretation of Results:
    • Current Status: Based on your calculations, is the project ahead or behind schedule? Is it over or under budget? Provide a clear explanation using the data.
    • Decision-Making: As the project manager, discuss the implications of your findings:
      • What risks do you see based on the current project performance?
      • What corrective actions (if any) would you recommend to ensure the project stays on track for the next three quarters?
      • What changes would you implement to recover if the project is behind schedule or over budget?
      • If the project is performing well, what strategies would you propose to maintain this performance or possibly improve efficiency further?
  4. Forecasting Future Performance:
    • Using your Estimate at Completion (EAC) and Variance at Completion (VAC) calculations, forecast the project's final Cost. Based on your analysis:
      • Will the project likely finish within the $200 million budget? If not, what is the projected overrun?
      • What changes to resource allocation, task management, or other project factors would you consider to mitigate any potential cost overruns or schedule delays?
  5. Preparation for the Review with the Vice President:
    Imagine you are presenting your findings to the vice president. Prepare a brief report that highlights the following:
    • The current status of the project (cost and schedule performance).
    • The projected completion costs and any expected budget overruns or savings.
    • A clear set of recommendations for the next steps, including corrective actions, risk mitigation strategies, or resource adjustments.
  6. MS Excel Submission:
    Use MS Excel to perform your EVM calculations and create visual aids (e.g., charts, tables) that help communicate your findings. Submit your Excel sheet alongside a report summarizing your analysis, recommendations, and forecast.
