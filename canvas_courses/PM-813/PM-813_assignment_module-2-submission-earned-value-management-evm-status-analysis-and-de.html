Objective:

In this exercise, you will perform Earned Value Management (EVM) calculations for a given project and, more importantly, interpret the results from a project manager's perspective. Your goal is to understand whether the project is on schedule and within budget and describe the potential actions you would take based on your findings.

Instructions:

  1. EVM Calculations:
    Based on the project data provided below, calculate the following for each task:
    • Planned Value (PV)
    • Earned Value (EV)
    • Cost Variance (CV)
    • Schedule Variance (SV)
    • Cost Performance Index (CPI)
    • Schedule Performance Index (SPI)

For the entire project, calculate:

    • Total PV, EV, and Actual Cost (AC)
    • Budget at Completion (BAC)
    • Cost Variance (CV) and Schedule Variance (SV)
    • Cost Performance Index (CPI) and Schedule Performance Index (SPI)
  1. Interpretation of Results:
    • Project Status: Based on your calculations, determine whether the project is ahead or behind schedule and over or under budget. Support your conclusions with data.
    • Decision-Making as a Project Manager: Reflect on your results. As a project manager, what actions would you consider taking to bring the project back on track if necessary?
      • If the project is behind schedule, how would you address delays?
      • If the project is over budget, what steps could be taken to control costs?
      • Would corrective actions be required immediately, or would you wait for more data? Explain your reasoning.
  2. Investigate Potential Causes:
    Identify any tasks that warrant further investigation based on your calculations and analysis. For instance, tasks with significant variances (positive or negative) may indicate more profound issues. Describe what aspects of these tasks you would investigate and why (e.g., resource allocation, scope changes, unforeseen risks).
  3. Use MS Excel for Calculations:
    Use MS Excel to organize your data, perform the calculations, and generate charts or visuals that help communicate your findings. Submit your Excel sheet and a brief report summarizing your key findings and recommended actions.
