Objective:

This assignment allows you to demonstrate your understanding of key project management concepts through the lens of effective communication. Whether you write a research paper or analyze a case study, you will reflect on decision-making, team dynamics, and the role of communication in achieving successful outcomes. You will also explore how communication strategies and decision-making influence project failures or successes.

Instructions:
You may submit either a research paper or a case study analysis for this assignment, but you must focus on integrating project management concepts, decision-making processes, and reflection.

Option 1: Research Paper (500-1000 words)

Task:
Reflect on an experience where you worked with a team to complete a project (e.g., a poster, presentation, document, or another collaborative task). In this paper, you will integrate project management concepts and decision-making strategies to analyze your team's performance and reflect on critical insights.

Questions to Address:

  1. Project Overview:
    • Briefly describe the project your team worked on, including the overall goal, the deliverables, and the timeline.
  2. Team Roles and Decision-Making:
    • What role did you play in the team, and how were tasks distributed among members? Explain the decision-making process within your team. Was it collaborative, or did one person take the lead? How did this impact the efficiency of the project?
  3. Communication Effectiveness:
    • How did your team communicate (meetings, emails, online tools)? What communication strategies were most effective in keeping the project on track? Were there any communication breakdowns, and how did you resolve them?
  4. Team Dynamics and Functioning:
    • Analyze how well your team functioned. Use project management concepts such as team development stages (forming, storming, norming, performing), conflict resolution, and collaboration. How did these elements affect the overall performance of the team?
  5. Outcome and Reflection:
    • Was the project outcome successful? Why or why not? Reflect on what went well and what could have been improved. Would you work with this team again, and what would you change regarding communication and decision-making if given the chance?
  6. Essential Project Management Insights:
    • What lessons did you learn from this experience about communication, leadership, and decision-making in a team environment? How will these lessons inform your future approaches to managing or participating in projects?

Option 2: Case Study Analysis (500-1000 words)

Task:
Analyze the Hyatt Regency Walkway Collapse case study. Use project management and communication concepts to identify what failed and recommend what could have been done differently to prevent the disaster. Hyatt Regency Walkway Collapse | Online Ethics

Questions to Address:

  1. Overview of the Case:
    • Provide a summary of the Hyatt Regency Walkway Collapse. What was the project's scope, and what were the critical decisions leading up to the disaster?
  2. Project Management Failures:
    • Analyze the project management failures that contributed to the disaster. Focus on leadership, decision-making, risk management, communication, and accountability. How did poor communication between stakeholders, engineers, and contractors contribute to the collapse?
  3. Communication and Decision-Making Analysis:
    • What role did ineffective communication play in the failure of the project? Discuss the communication breakdowns among project stakeholders and how they affected decision-making and risk management. Could better communication have prevented the disaster?
  4. Risk Management Insights:
    • Identify the key risks that were overlooked or poorly managed. How could a more proactive approach to risk management have changed the outcome? Discuss how incorporating risk identification, assessment, and mitigation strategies could have improved decision-making.
  5. Preventative Measures and Recommendations:
    • Based on your analysis, what corrective actions should have been taken at critical points in the project to avoid the collapse? Discuss how better communication strategies, project oversight, and decision-making could have prevented the failure. What lessons can modern project managers learn from this case?
  6. Essential Project Management Insights:
    • Reflect on the lessons learned from this case regarding communication, leadership, and project execution. How can these lessons be applied to future projects to avoid similar failures?
