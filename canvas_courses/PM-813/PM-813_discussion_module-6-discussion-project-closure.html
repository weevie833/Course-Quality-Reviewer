An organization may have its own internal process that would guide the project closeout activities, or the project manager may need to define the process. Closeout activities must also be performed for projects that are terminated early.

Improperly closed projects can lead to issues and in some cases financial loses.

Example 1: A PM forgetting to close a charge number for a completed project and someone inadvertently charging to it for several months afterward.  While it was easy to correct when discovered, it was a nuisance.

Example 2: The company where the PM worked was a contractor who had a fixed price bid on a project with slim margins.  Three months after the project was completed, someone found equipment stored away that had been leased for that project.  The company had been paying the lease on that equipment the entire three months, which wiped out their profits.
