Procurement Management includes the processes necessary to purchase or acquire goods or services for the project. The procurement management plan documents the procurement approach and describes how the process will be managed. A procurement management plan can be formal or informal, can be highly detailed or broadly framed, and is based on the needs of each project. 

For this assignment, you will begin to create a Procurement Management Plan for your capstone/ party project. This is a key artifact in your Programmatic ePortfolio.  Although not all projects have a procurement component, you will need to create one as if your project requires purchase of goods or services. (Assume you may need to use contract resources, consulting services, or other types of goods or services likely needed for you’re your project.) Your submission may address the following components:

  • Funding Sources
  • Types of contract(s)
  • Risk Management Issues
  • Evaluation Criteria, and scoring system
  • Integration with the organization’s purchasing department
  • Standardized procurement documents, if any
  • Management of multiple suppliers procedures
  • Constraints and Assumptions
  • Coordination with the project schedule / handling long lead times
  • Payment and/or Performance bonds and/or insurance requirements
  • Identification of pre-qualified sellers if any
  • Performance metrics to manage contracts and sellers

This is an ungraded assignment. The final graded version of the procurement management plan is due  in Week 6. You may choose to submit a draft / partial version of the document this week and incorporate any feedback.

You may use the template provided or use one of your own.
