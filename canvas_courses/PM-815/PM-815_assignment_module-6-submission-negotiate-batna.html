As project managers, we are often faced with difficult negotiations for goods or services that are critical to the successful completion of our project. A critical planning exercise when preparing for a negotiation is to determine what is your Best Alternative to a negotiated agreement (BATNA). The term BATNA was coined by Roger Fisher and William Ury in their book: Getting to Yes: Negotiating Without Giving In. BATNA is basically the best you can walk away with when the other party refuses to negotiate or when your negotiations fail. In order to prepare for your negotiation, you need to understand what is the minimum outcome you are able to live with. The BATNA will provide you the true measure against which you can evaluate any proposed agreement. It is the only standard which can protect you both from accepting terms that are too unfavorable and from rejecting terms it would be in your interest to accept. (Roger Fisher and William Ury, Getting to Yes [Penguin Books, 1991], 100-01)
