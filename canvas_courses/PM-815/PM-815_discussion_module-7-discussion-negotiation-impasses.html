Sometimes negotiations can become contentious to the point of breakdown. In extreme cases, conflict escalates, disputes can arise and interpersonal relations can become strained and even nasty. Or worse yet, you can become stuck in the mud, essentially at impasse....

We would call this a "lose-lose", where each side has compromised so much, that what they intended to get out of the session has been so diluted, they walk away feeling like it was a loss....

There are mistakes that negotiators can make that increase the likelihood of negotiation impasses...

  • Neglecting the other side's problems
  • Focusing on positions over interests
  • Too much focus on common ground
  • Neglecting BATNA's
  • Adjusting perceptions during the negotiations
  • Not proactively managing the process itself

Pick ONE of the mistakes mentioned above (try to pick one not previously mentioned by another student) and talk about what it means, how or why it might happen, and how you might deal with it if YOU saw it happening in your negotiation.
