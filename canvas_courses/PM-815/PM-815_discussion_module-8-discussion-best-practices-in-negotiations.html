I wanted to put it all together with one last question on the best practices in negotiation.

Negotiation is an integral part of your daily life, and of our project management lives, as the opportunities to negotiate surround us. While some people may look like born negotiators, negotiation is fundamentally a skill involving analysis and communication that everyone can learn.

And I hope you HAVE LEARNED throughout this course to appreciate the extent to which negotiation is both an art and science.

For this forum, I would like you to pick ONE of the BEST PRACTICES listed below and share your thoughts and perspectives learned from this course in 1-2 paragraphs. No research, but of course, you can research if you want to (please cite references if you do)....just your opinions on what you have learned. There are no additional posts required for this forum.

  1. Be Prepared
  2. Diagnose the Fundamental Structure of the Negotiation
  3. Identify and Work the BATNA
  4. Be Willing to Walk Away
  5. Master the Paradoxes of Negotiation - i.e. being too trusting vs. being too distrusting; being too honest vs. being too opaque, sticking w/ your strategy vs. exploring new options, etc
  6. Remember the Intangibles - those deep psychological factors like winning, avoiding loss, not looking weak, being fair, impressing the boss, etc...
  7. Actively Manage Coalitions - those against you, those for you, and the unknowns
  8. Savor and Protect Your Reputation
  9. Remember that Rationality and Fairness are Relative
  10. Continue to Learn from your Experience

Looking forward to hearing your opinions!
