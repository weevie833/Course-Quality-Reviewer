PM817: Managing Project Portfolios

This course addresses the processes and techniques used in the strategic management of project portfolios. You will examine decision-making tools, techniques, and the rationale used to reach consensus for funding specific programs and projects, and to bring them into the tactical layer for execution. The course reviews objective methodologies, benefit measurement techniques, market analytics, competitive analysis and market-driven approaches.

In this course we will discuss the value of Project Portfolio Management (PPM) as well as how PPM fits in and supports project management and organizational strategy in general. When done well, PPM ensures that those projects selected for implementation within the organization map to the organization's long term strategic goals.

We'll also example how to manage risks to the project portfolio. And, while there are certainly any number of potential risks, we'll consider risks from the perspective of a situation such as the recent Covid-19 pandemic.

Please bring your own experiences and stories into the discussions as we learn not just from the materials presented but also from each other. Have a problem to discuss?  Let's use the knowledge of the group to problem solve it!
