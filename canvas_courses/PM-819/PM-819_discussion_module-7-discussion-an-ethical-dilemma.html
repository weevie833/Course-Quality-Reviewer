Consider the following situation and share your thoughts with one another. What would you have done if faced with this situation?

You are the project manager on a multi-million-dollar construction project. The project is nearing completion and so far, you and your team of contractors have done an admirable job of staying relatively on schedule and within the original budget.

In a recent discussion with one of your contractors, it was brought to your attention that some of the project engineers had expressed concern about the materials being used. It would appear the bolts used in anchoring roof tiles were not of a consistent size or quality. One large shipment of bolts was not in alignment with the specifications by a 1/4th of an inch in length. When this was brought to the attention of the contract supervisor, the team was informed “That is okay. The rest of the roof would hold it in place.” 

When further asked, your contractor informed you that re-ordering that shipment, which was sourced from off-shore, would delay the project by three weeks at a minimum, and more likely six weeks. They felt their supervisor had made the right decision in that it was important to stay on schedule and not incur extra material costs.

What actions should you as the project manager undertake at this point? As you think about your optional actions, consider how your readings and media influenced your approach. Discuss the factors you should take into consideration when determining what to do with this new information.
