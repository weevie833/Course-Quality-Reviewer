Overview

Last week you were introduced to quality management and the crucial role it plays in a successful project or business.  But, have you ever heard an executive say: “I know it is not a quality product, but it is good enough based on the market.”  Or “I know they are not quite the right consultant for the assignment, but they are available. They will just need to learn on the fly.”  Or something similar?  How do those statements make you, the individual responsible for your organization’s quality management program, feel? 

This week you will look into how current ethical practices and cultures influence decisions made regarding the quality of a product or a service. Customer satisfaction and methods by which to ascertain how they feel about your organization and the work it performs. You will want to keep these possible attitudes and resistance in mind as you develop your quality management plan; you will need to design approaches to overcome them.
