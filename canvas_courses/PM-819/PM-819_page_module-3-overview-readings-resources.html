Overview

Implementing a quality management program within a project or an organization takes thoughtful planning. Chances are the implementation will be a significant organizational change for the employees and team members. 

Quality management is not just focused on products and services. It is also about the processes used by the organization. High-quality processes are typically lean, well-documented, and part of a continuous improvement program.

Thought needs to be given as to how your quality plans will be applied to the various vendors and sub-contractors supporting your organization. Can certain quality metrics be identified during negotiations and then embedded into contracts as a condition of contract completion? Sometimes quality is not only about the contract’s final delivery but also about the contractor’s processes, for instance timely and accurate invoicing.

This week you will learn what should be considered in your quality management plan for the organization and how to address the challenges associated with putting that plan into action.
