Overview

Following World War II, several countries around the globe realized they needed to reconstruct their economies, to shift from war-time production to consumer-goods production.  One of those countries, Japan, recognized quality could be a significant market differentiator for their goods, as they entered into market competition with  European manufacturers. Their efforts in this area have evolved to what is now called “total quality management.”

This week’s readings will introduce you to a globally accepted standard for quality, ISO 9000, and its relationship to total quality management. You will also be introduced to a variety of tools used in quality management, tools such as Pareto charts and Cause-and-Effect diagrams.
