Overview

The video below shows the cost of businesses failing to perform the necessary tests to prevent costly disasters.   While it talks to business managers, it could just as easily be talking to project managers who don’t thoroughly review the quality of the project’s deliverables.  I can only imagine how the NASA Project Manager overseeing the Challenger must have felt, knowing they might have been able to prevent it by insisting on one more test.  

Oftentimes, when faced with a quality issue, managers need to make tough decisions. Do they delay the release of a new software feature that is not market-ready, knowing that to do so is the right thing for their customers but not for their shareholders?  More recently, we have the quandary Boeing has found itself in on its 380MAX aircraft.  What was wrong in their quality management processes that permitted airframes to be released missing crucial bolts?  Surely someone noticed this flaw, but did not speak up.  We ask why ...fear of retribution for making management aware of something that was going to delay release of the aircraft to customers?

The video you will watch this week closes with a quote from Henry Ford: "Quality means doing it right when no one else is looking."   In other words,  total quality management requires ethical behavior on the parts of all participants.
