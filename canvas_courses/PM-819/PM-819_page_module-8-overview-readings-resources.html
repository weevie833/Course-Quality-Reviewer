Overview

Closely related to quality management are the concepts associated with continuous improvement.  When a product, service, or process has been deemed of “quality” it is important to establish a process by which it is assessed for continuation, improvement, or replacement. As a business matures, the processes it used in its infancy perhaps no longer serve it well.  The same evolution happens in the marketplace into which the business places its products and services. Businesses need to stay on top of what is happening outside their boundaries and assess the overall quality, the fitness, of what they offer.

You will explore the concepts associated with the more prevalent means by which businesses improve: implementing “Lean,” applying “Six Sigma” and establishing “Benchmarking” as a normal business practice.
