Welcome to PM 830 – Contemporary Topics in IT Project Management

PM 830 is designed to challenge IT Project Managers to expand their skills in managing complex IT projects, with a focus on DevOps practices, software development life cycles (SDLCs), AI risk/benefit analysis, and effective requirements management. Emphasis will be placed on how to develop a project life cycle model that fits the needs of the project at hand, drawing from several industry frameworks. Through dialog, case studies, experiential analysis, and practical application scenarios, students will design strategies for continuous integration and delivery in a fast-paced, agile environment. PREREQUISITE: PM 800, Introduction to Project Management.

In this course, students will expand their project management knowledge to encompass the unique requirements of managing IT projects in today’s complex environment. Students will explore the various project management and software development life cycle models needed to develop a project life cycle model that supports the value proposition associated with the system (hardware, network, data and software) they have been authorized to produce. Concepts associated with DevOps, continuous delivery, and quality control will be analyzed through the lens of emerging technology.
