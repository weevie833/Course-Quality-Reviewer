Overview

This module explores the process-driven nature of DevOps and its impact on IT project management. DevOps is more than a methodology—it is a cultural and operational shift that integrates development, operations, and automation to enhance software delivery speed, reliability, and efficiency. Students will examine DevOps as a continuous, iterative process that streamlines development and operations through CI/CD pipelines, infrastructure as code (IaC), automated testing, and real-time monitoring. They will also analyze how DevOps interacts with IT Service Management (ITSM) and ITIL frameworks, ensuring that rapid development aligns with service reliability, incident resolution, and governance best practices.

Key topics include:

  • Foundations of DevOps – Understanding the principles, benefits, and key drivers behind DevOps adoption.
  • The DevOps Pipeline – Examining CI/CD, automation, and feedback loops for continuous improvement.
  • Infrastructure as Code (IaC) and Automation – How tools like Terraform and Ansible redefine infrastructure management.
  • ITSM and ITIL in a DevOps Environment – Balancing rapid development with structured service management and governance.
  • Change and Incident Management – Ensuring stability while accelerating software delivery.
  • Security and Compliance in DevOps (DevSecOps) – Embedding security within automated workflows.

By the end of this module, students will have a clear understanding of DevOps processes, their implementation, and their integration with ITSM and ITIL. Whether or not students have prior knowledge of DevOps, they will gain practical insights into how modern IT project management leverages automation, continuous feedback, and service management frameworks to optimize software delivery and operations.
