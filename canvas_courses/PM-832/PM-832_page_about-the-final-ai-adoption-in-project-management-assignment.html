There are two parts to the final assignment:

Part 1: A written scholarly analysis of the business case that supports an initiative to strategically adopt and integrate AI into project management practices in your organization or in an organization with which you are familiar (former employer, military, etc.). The intent of the written scholarly analysis is to produce the substantive argument according to the topics covered in the course and the scholarly literature that meets graduate-level standards for research and communication. This is the research you will need to develop as a basis of producing the video presentation in Part 2.

Part 2: A video presentation that conveys your case to decision-makers in the context of an executive-level assembly. The video presentation tests your ability to synthesize complex scholarly research and present a clear narrative to a non-technical audience, in plain language and visualizations. Your goal is to be persuasive through the use of substantive, empirical evidence rather than hearsay or “hype” so that your stakeholders and sponsors can make informed decisions.

You will be assessed according to your ability to synthesize course concepts, apply them to real-world organizational contexts, and communicate your insights both professionally and persuasively. Carefully review the requirements for each part of this assignment.

Part I: Scholarly Research (5–7 pages)

Produce a scholarly analysis of your organization’s PM practice through the lens of AI-based tools’ potential to impact growth and efficacy. Include the following:

  1. An introduction to the context of your organization’s PM practice.
  2. An assessment of the potential for AI-based tools to augment PM practice within the organization. Be specific about the area(s) that are most relevant and why it matters. Describe the potential outcome(s) of your initiative.
  3. Identify the first key area of interest for your business case. Describe its current state, then develop the argument for its transformation through the use of AI-based tools. Support your position through scholarly resources and evidence.
  4. Continue this pattern of analysis (current state, argumentation, supportive evidence) for all other key areas of interest as you have identified them, inclusive of all phases of the PM process.
  5. Develop a practical governance checklist or framework for your organization that addresses transparency, accountability, data quality, bias mitigation, and human oversight.
  6. Describe the ethical responsibilities incumbent to AI adoption, balancing innovation with organizational values, stakeholder trust, and long-term sustainability. If applicable, offer a substantive argument that defends not using AI for certain tasks or responsibilities.
  7. Offer a summative conclusion that relates to the outcomes promised in the introduction and which offers guidance for taking specific next-step actions.

Requirements:

  • Length: 5-7 pages (excluding references)
  • Format: APA 7th Edition
  • Minimum of five scholarly or professional sources
Note: This assignment is designated Level 1 - No AI. The assignment must be completed entirely without AI assistance. The assignment must be produced relying solely on conventional research and study methods. AI must not be used at any point during the production of the assignment.
