Course Management Guidelines

  • Weekly Engagement: Ensure students post one original discussion response and two peer replies each week. Prompt follow-up questions to deepen engagement.
  • Assignment Feedback: Provide rubric-based feedback within one week of submission. Highlight both AI tool application and critical reflection, not just output.
  • Academic Integrity: Emphasize responsible use of AI. Remind students that AI tools must support—not replace—their original analysis.

Technology Setup & Tools

  1. AI Tools in Use
    • ChatGPT, Notion AI, Wrike AI, Smartsheet AI, ClickUp AI, RiskLens, Predict!
  2. Library & Writing Support
    • Direct students to UNH Library for scholarly sources beyond AI tools.

Instructor Best Practices

  • Model AI Use: Early in the course, demonstrate how to use AI responsibly (e.g., generating ideas, drafting outlines, testing scenarios).
  • Balance Critique and Encouragement: Since this is a fast-evolving field, validate student experimentation while guiding them to think critically about risks, ethics, and limitations.
  • Diversity of Examples: Encourage students to explore AI applications across industries (IT, healthcare, finance, construction) to broaden perspectives.
  • Scaffolded Learning: Remind students how each module connects — Charter (Module 2) → WBS (Module 3) → Schedule (Module 4) → Cost/Risk/Stakeholders (Modules 5–7) → Ethics & Leadership (Module 8).

Instructor Notes by Module

  • Module 1: Set tone with discussion on opportunities/challenges of AI.
  • Module 2: Reinforce that the charter is foundational — encourage thoughtful use of AI for stakeholder identification.
  • Module 3–4: Remind students that WBS feeds directly into scheduling; emphasize dependency management.
  • Midterm: Ensure students integrate reflection with deliverables; not just submit standalone documents.
  • Module 5–6: Highlight ethical concerns around cost/risk predictions. Push students to critique models, not just use them.
  • Module 7–8: Guide debates on communication ethics, data privacy, and AI governance. Encourage connections to professional codes of conduct (e.g., PMI).
  • Final Project: Stress that analysis and integration matter as much as the technical plan.
