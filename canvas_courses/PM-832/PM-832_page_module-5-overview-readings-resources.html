Overview

In this module, you will examine how AI enhances cost estimating and budgeting processes in project management. You will explore how AI tools analyze historical project data, market conditions, and resource usage patterns to generate more accurate forecasts. At the same time, you will critically reflect on AI’s limitations—such as dataset bias—and the vital role of human oversight.

Building on the integrated work from your Midterm Portfolio, this module marks the start of the course’s second half, focused on financial considerations. The insights you gain here will lay the groundwork for Module 6, where AI’s capabilities in risk management and predictive analytics will be further explored.

Readings

  • Chapter 6: People-centric AI (approximately 13 pages)
  • PMI, A Guide to the Project Management Body of Knowledge (PMBOK® Guide), 7th Edition (2021). Selected :

      • Estimate Costs – techniques for preparing accurate estimates. Pages: 155–158

      • Earned Value Management (EVM) – integrating scope, schedule, and cost to assess performance. Pages: 242–244
    • Insight into EVM components (EV, PV, ACWP, variances) and their significance in cost control

  • Case Study: How AI Streamlines Construction Cost Analysis and Budget Automation - This blog post by Datagrid explores how AI revolutionizes construction cost management by integrating data connectors and Agentic AI. It highlights key advantages such as:

    • Streamlined workflows
    • Minimized errors
    • Enhanced cost estimation accuracy
    • Real-time insights from varied data sources (e.g., labor logs, machine data, weather forecasts)
