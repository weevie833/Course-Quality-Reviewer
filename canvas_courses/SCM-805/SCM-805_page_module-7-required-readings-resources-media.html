Overview

Just-in-time (JIT) has been alluded to throughout the module, and we finally see its full treatment here in Module 11. Learners should continue to emphasize that JIT is a philosophy of continuous problem solving that drives out waste. It is not a set of techniques or just something about kanbans or small lot sizes. JIT is articulated as a journey toward a long-term goal of working in a completely lean operation. For example, pure JIT might suggest lot sizes of one unit, but in practice, we more often think of moving toward JIT and reducing lot sizes 

Learning Objectives 

  1. Define lean operations 
  2. Define the seven wastes of the 5Ss 
  3. Identify the concerns of supplier when moving to supplier partnerships
