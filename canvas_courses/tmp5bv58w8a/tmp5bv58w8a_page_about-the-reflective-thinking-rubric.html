This course includes an assignment that will be assessed according to how well you demonstrate Reflective Thinking. As the words “reflective thinking” imply, your work should show an account of important events in your learning experience that demonstrate critical thinking about how your learning occurred.

Definition of Reflective Thinking:  Reflective thinking is the ability to apply the techniques of critical inquiry in describing how changes in knowledge, skills, or attitudes occurred over time. There are four levels of Reflective Thinking, each corresponding to the degree of inquiry you have applied to your learning experiences. Your work will be assessed according to the presence or absence of the indicators at each level in the model below.

  • Level I - Descriptive Writing: A description of events that occurred presented as a report but without evidence of a perspective. No attempt to provide reasons or justifications for events.
  • Level II - Descriptive Reflection: A description of events that occurred presented as a report with some evidence of a perspective, but only at a surface level of inquiry to explain the reasons or justifications for events.<
  • Level III - Dialogic Reflection: A demonstration of “stepping back” from the events and actions in the learning process leading to an interrogative discourse with oneself. The author explores the experiences, events, and actions using qualitative judgment and considers possible alternatives for explaining outcomes; identifies connections, cause/effect, and patterns in their rationales and critiques.
  • Level IV - Critical Reflection: Includes all the elements of Dialogic Reflection but also takes into account the larger/global social, scholarly, and professional context in which events occur; considers perspectives other than one’s own.
