Programmatic Learning Outcomes (PLO) for Master's Degree in Leadership-Human Resources

PLO 1: Formulate flexible methods of leadership to adapt to changing conditions in organizations, situations, cultures, and external events.
PLO 2: Evaluate organizational relationships and their ability to contribute to organizational effectiveness.
PLO 3: Develop change management methods informed by evidence-based leadership theories that enable an organization to achieve its goals and mission.
PLO 4: Develop an ethical framework for implementing a leadership model consistent with an organization's mission, goals, and culture.
PLO 5: Assess the field of human resource management and its relevance to leadership practices in organizations.
PLO 6: Integrate HR concepts to build a competent, committed workforce, and a flexible, integrated, cost-effective organization.

================================================================================
REQUIRED COURSES
================================================================================

HRM-805 Managing Human Resources in a Global Economy
CLO 1: Analyze HR practices and the impact on organizational performance.
CLO 2: Evaluate methods for recruiting, developing, and retaining talent that align with both business objectives and the SHRM Competency Model.
CLO 3: Analyze performance management systems to assess and improve employee performance aligned with organizational goals.
CLO 4: Utilize workforce planning and data analytics to make informed decisions that support the strategic direction of the organization.
CLO 5: Develop strategies for HR's role in managing organizational change, fostering innovation, and promoting agility in response to market dynamics.
CLO 6: Propose strategies for enhancing employee engagement, motivation, and retention to cultivate organizational culture and achievement.
CLO 7: Design HR systems that promote a fair, equitable workplace in compliance with SHRM-based Ethical Codes.

HRM-810 Business Acumen: Role of HR in Business
CLO 1: Evaluate operations, quality management techniques, and Human Resources strategies to drive organizational improvement.
CLO 2: Analyze accounting data and Human Resources metrics to effectively manage and optimize organizational resources.
CLO 3: Utilize information systems and Human Resources technologies to enhance productivity and operational efficiency.
CLO 4: Apply economic principles and Human Resources analysis to address and solve business challenges.
CLO 5: Analyze marketing strategies and Human Resources initiatives to achieve competitive advantage in the marketplace.
CLO 6: Use financial and Human Resources analysis to inform and support key business decisions.

HRM-815 Employment Law and Ethics
CLO 1: Interpret how federal, state, and international regulations impact business operations, compliance with laws governing employment, consumer protection, and environmental standards.
CLO 2: Communicate complex legal and ethical issues in plain language to stakeholders so that they can make informed business decisions.
CLO 3: Investigate various forms of conflict and propose professional methods of dispute resolution, negotiation, mediation, and arbitration, to resolve them.
CLO 4: Assess the legal and ethical implications of cultural differences, international laws, and global supply chains for businesses operating in a global context.
CLO 5: Analyze the legal and ethical challenges faced by businesses operating in a global context.
CLO 6: Develop employment practices aligned with federal and state employment laws and statues.

HRM-830 HR Technology and People Analytics
CLO 1: Illustrate how Human Resource Information Systems (HRIS) and other technologies manage and automate HR processes.
CLO 2: Utilize people analytics to make informed decisions relevant to achieving talent management, workforce planning, and organizational performance goals.
CLO 3: Interpret complex HR data and present findings to stakeholders in plain language so that leadership can make informed decisions about strategic HR initiatives.
CLO 4: Employ HR applications, platforms, digital tools, and people analytics according to legal, ethical, and privacy frameworks, ensuring compliance with relevant regulations.
CLO 5: Utilize HR analytics, applications, and digital tools to forecast workforce needs, optimize employee development, and improve retention strategies.

LD-804 Leading Teams
CLO 1: Develop a leadership guide using strategies that foster team culture within a course project.
CLO 2: Adapt conflict resolution techniques as applicable when leading teams.
CLO 3: Critique a leader's source and types of power within a team.
CLO 4: Evaluate how team structures can impact the leader's responsibility and authority levels.

LD-820 Cultivating Your Leadership Capabilities
CLO 1: Assess the characteristics of effective leaders and paradigms of leadership.
CLO 2: Develop an action plan for growth based upon personal leadership competencies identified in a leadership instrument.
CLO 3: Examine leadership skills, styles, and traits and their influence on followers.

LD-823 Emergence of a Strategic Leader
CLO 1: Examine strategic concepts and tools as they apply to becoming a strategic leader.
CLO 2: Evaluate industry dynamics, competitive challenges, and performance from the position of a leader.
CLO 3: Apply strategic tools to a personal leadership development plan based upon individual strategic leader competencies.

MGMT-805 Organizational Behavior
CLO 1: Identify key theories, models, and management styles as found in an organizational setting.
CLO 2: Analyze an organization and make behavioral recommendations for development and effectiveness by utilizing critical thinking skills.
CLO 3: Apply theories and concepts related to the decision-making process in various organizational settings.
CLO 4: Demonstrate the ability to conduct research and formulate a synthesized literature review to form conclusions on a specified topic related to organizational behavior.
CLO 5: Establish a team-oriented goal that requires communication, inclusion, problem-solving, decision-making, and implementation strategies.
CLO 6: Demonstrate the ability to comprehend and articulate key concepts relating to both individuals and groups in an organizational context.

LD-850 Leadership Integrative Capstone

================================================================================
ELECTIVE COURSES
================================================================================

HRM-820 Recruitment and Selection
CLO 1: Design recruitment, selection, and retention methods that align to organizational objectives and competitive goals.
CLO 2: Develop strategies for workforce planning and assessing skills gaps as they relate to demographic changes and evolving labor market trends.
CLO 3: Formulate the optimal relationship between the strategic design of recruitment/selection and the cost of its implementation.
CLO 4: Propose industry supported decision-making frameworks to implement workforce planning initiatives for recruitment, development, and retention that meet organizational needs.
CLO 5: Integrate federal, state, and local regulations into the design of organizational recruitment, selection, and retention strategies and policies.
CLO 6: Develop a process model for assessing candidate qualifications and creating commensurate job offer packages.

HRM-821 Strategic Rewards and Performance Management
CLO 1: Design reward and compensation strategies that align with organizational goals and drive employee motivation.
CLO 2: Integrate performance management systems within broader business strategies to achieve productivity and organizational performance goals.
CLO 3: Integrate relevant legal standards and ethical considerations into the design of rewards and performance management systems.
CLO 4: Develop viable strategies for managing rewards and performance in a global or culturally diverse workforce.

HRM-822 Talent Management and Development
CLO 1: Assess organizational and employee training needs according to business objectives.
CLO 2: Propose a comprehensive training program incorporating instructional design principles that address the specific knowledge and skills required to achieve business objectives.
CLO 3: Propose instructional strategies and delivery modes appropriate to specific skill development and knowledge transfer into authentic conditions.
CLO 4: Utilize evaluation models to assess the effectiveness of training programs on both individual and organizational performance.
CLO 5: Develop strategies to assure trainees' transfer of newly acquired skills and knowledge to perform with higher productivity in the workplace.
CLO 6: Develop processes and methods for new hire onboarding, facilitating acculturation into the organization, and orientation.
